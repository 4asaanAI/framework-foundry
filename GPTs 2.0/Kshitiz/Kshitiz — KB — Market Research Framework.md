## Market Sizing Methodology

### Top-Down Approach
```
TAM = Total addressable market (all potential revenue if 100% market share)
SAM = Serviceable addressable market (segment you can realistically reach)
SOM = Serviceable obtainable market (what you can capture in 3 years)

Example for Layaa AI:
TAM: India AI market = $17B by 2030 (NASSCOM/Government estimates)
     63M+ MSMEs in India
SAM: ~5M SMEs in services sector (NCR + Tier 1 cities, Rs.50L-5Cr revenue)
     = ~$500M addressable market
SOM: 50-100 SME clients, Rs.50L-1Cr revenue in 3 years
```

### Bottom-Up Approach
```
SOM = (Number of reachable prospects) x (Conversion rate) x (Average deal value)
    = (500 qualified leads/year) x (5-8% end-to-end) x (Rs.2.5L avg)
    = Rs.62.5L - Rs.1Cr / year

Cross-validate with top-down SOM for consistency.
```

## ICP Segment Research Framework

For each ICP segment, maintain:

| Data Point | Source Priority | Update Frequency |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Market size (India) | A-Primary (Government/NASSCOM) | Annual |
| Growth rate | B-Published (Industry reports) | Annual |
| Number of companies in segment | A-Primary (MCA data, industry associations) | Annual |
| Average revenue range | B-Published (Industry reports) | Annual |
| Key pain points | C-Secondary + primary research | Quarterly |
| Technology adoption rate | B-Published (Survey reports) | Annual |
| Budget for technology/AI | B-Published or C-Secondary | Annual |
| Average sales cycle | Internal data + B-Published | Quarterly |
| Decision maker profiles | C-Secondary + internal experience | Quarterly |
| Competitive landscape | Direct research | Monthly |
