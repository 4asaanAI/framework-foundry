## Research Process (6 Steps)

1. **Define** — What specific question needs answering? What decision will this research inform? Who is the requesting agent?
2. **Scope** — What data is needed? What is in/out of scope? What is the time constraint?
3. **Collect** — Gather data from sources ranked by reliability (A-Primary, B-Published, C-Secondary). Prefer Indian-market-specific data.
4. **Analyze** — Apply appropriate statistical/analytical methods. Look for patterns, outliers, and contradictions.
5. **Interpret** — Translate findings into Layaa AI-specific context. What does this mean for our ICP, pricing, positioning, or operations?
6. **Deliver** — Present findings with evidence tags, source citations, confidence levels, and clear limitations.

## Research Types and When to Use Each

| Research Type | When to Use | Output |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| **Market Sizing** | New market entry, investor deck, strategic planning | TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology and sources |
| **Competitive Analysis** | Battle card creation, positioning review, pricing benchmarking | Competitor profiles, feature comparison, pricing matrix |
| **Industry Benchmarking** | KPI target setting, performance assessment, proposal support | Benchmark database by ICP segment |
| **Trend Analysis** | Strategy planning, content creation support, product roadmap | Trend reports with trajectory and implications |
| **Claim Verification** | Validating any agent's factual assertions | Validation result with source and confidence |
| **Account Research** | Pre-sales, discovery prep, client onboarding | Company profile, pain points, decision makers, budget signals |
| **Regulatory Intelligence** | Supporting Preeti with regulatory landscape data | Enforcement trends, compliance adoption rates |

## The MECE Framework for Research Scoping

Ensure research is **Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive**:
- Break the research question into non-overlapping sub-questions
- Verify that all sub-questions together answer the full question
- Eliminate duplicate research paths
- Identify gaps where no sub-question covers a needed area
