### 1. Aaryans School -- EduFlow

**Status:** Active client. This is our flagship engagement and proof of concept.

**Details:**
- Aaryans School operates 2 branches.
- We are delivering EduFlow -- an AI-powered school administration automation system.
- The relationship is live and generating revenue (Aarav handles the financial side).

**What I Know About This Client:**
- Schools are operationally chaotic. Admissions, fee collection, attendance, parent communication, report cards, transport management -- all of it is either manual or running on disconnected software that the admin staff barely uses.
- Aaryans came to us because the pain of managing 2 branches with manual processes was becoming unsustainable. The principal/owner was spending more time on admin than on education.
- EduFlow addresses this by automating the repetitive admin workflows. The value proposition is time savings and error reduction, not fancy technology.

**Lessons From This Engagement:**
- Schools buy on trust and relationship, not on feature lists. The decision-maker is usually the owner/principal, and they need to feel that you understand their world.
- Pricing sensitivity is high. Schools operate on thin margins, especially in the Rs.3,000-8,000/month fee bracket. Our pricing needs to reflect this.
- Implementation needs to be dead simple. School admin staff are not tech-savvy. If it requires training beyond 30 minutes, adoption will fail.

## Discovery Insights by Industry

### Education (Schools)

**Core Pain Points:**
1. Admissions management is a nightmare during season (Feb-April). Forms, follow-ups, seat allocation, fee negotiation -- all manual.
2. Parent communication is fragmented. WhatsApp groups are chaotic, circulars get lost, fee reminders are awkward.
3. Fee collection and tracking is the biggest financial pain. Schools lose 10-15% of revenue to delayed or defaulted fees simply because follow-up is inconsistent.
4. Multi-branch management multiplies every problem. The owner cannot be in two places at once, and branch managers often lack the tools for independent decision-making.

**What Schools Actually Want:**
- "I want to open my phone in the morning and see a dashboard that tells me everything is okay, or exactly what needs my attention."
- They do not want software. They want peace of mind. Frame everything around outcomes, not features.

### CA Firms

**Core Pain Points:**
1. Document collection from clients is painful. Chasing clients for Form 16, bank statements, investment proofs -- this eats weeks of time.
2. Data entry from documents into Tally or ITR software is repetitive and error-prone.
3. Compliance calendar management -- missing a client's filing deadline is a professional liability.
4. Client communication at scale -- sending status updates, collecting signatures, issuing acknowledgments.

**What CAs Actually Want:**
- Speed during peak season. If they can file 500 ITRs instead of 300 in the same window, that is direct revenue growth.
- Accuracy guarantees. They will not adopt anything that might introduce errors. Confidence in the output is non-negotiable.

## ICP Engagement Patterns

**What Works:**
- Warm introductions and referrals convert at 3-5x the rate of cold outreach. Every happy client should be asked for referrals systematically.
- Demo-first conversations. Do not sell with slides. Show the product doing something relevant to their world within the first 5 minutes.
- Owner/principal-level engagement. Do not waste time with middle management in SMEs. The decision-maker is the owner, and they decide fast if they see value.
- WhatsApp as a communication channel. Indian SME owners live on WhatsApp. Email is for formal documents only.

**What Does Not Work:**
- Long sales cycles with multiple presentations. If they are not ready to commit after 2 meetings, they are either not in enough pain or we are talking to the wrong person.
- Technical jargon. Never say "LLM," "RAG," "fine-tuning" to a school principal. Say "the system reads the document and fills in the form for you."
- Competing on features against established players. We win on simplicity, speed of deployment, and personal attention -- not on having more buttons.

## Common Client Concerns

These come up repeatedly and I need ready answers:

| Concern | Our Response Framework |

| **Cost** | "What is the cost of NOT automating? Calculate the hours your staff spends on X, multiply by their salary. We pay for ourselves in month one." |

## Handoff Protocol to Rohit

Rohit handles solution design and delivery after I qualify and close the client. Here is what a clean handoff looks like:

**What Rohit Needs From Me:**
1. **Client Brief:** Company name, contact person, industry, size (employees, branches, revenue range if known).
2. **Pain Points:** Ranked by urgency. What keeps them up at night? What triggered them to look for a solution now?
3. **Scope Agreement:** What exactly did we agree to deliver in the first phase? Be specific -- "automate admissions workflow for 2 branches" not "build an education platform."
4. **Budget & Terms:** What did we quote? What payment terms did they agree to? (50% advance, 50% delivery is standard -- confirm with Aarav.)
5. **Timeline Expectations:** When does the client expect to see something working? Flag any aggressive timelines.
6. **Relationship Notes:** Who is the day-to-day contact vs. the decision-maker? Any personality notes (eg. "prefers WhatsApp voice notes over email," "very data-driven, wants weekly metrics").
7. **Red Flags:** Anything that worried me during discovery. Unrealistic expectations, price pushback, unclear internal buy-in.

I do not throw clients over the wall. I stay involved through the first 2 weeks of delivery to make sure the transition is smooth and the client does not feel abandoned.
