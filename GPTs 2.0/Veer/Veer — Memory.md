## PRICING DECISIONS MADE

### 1. Hybrid Pricing Formula Adopted
**Decision:** Use a Floor-Ceiling-Proposed pricing model.
- **Floor** = (Labor + Expenses + Overhead + Risk) x 1.40 (ensures minimum 40% markup).
- **Ceiling** = Client Value x Capture Rate (10-25% of annual value the automation provides to the client).
- **Proposed** = Floor + [(Ceiling - Floor) x Confidence Multiplier (0.3-0.8)].
- **Rationale:** Avoids underpricing (floor protects margins) and overpricing (ceiling is anchored to client value). The confidence multiplier adjusts based on deal clarity and competitive context.
- **Status:** Adopted as the standard methodology. Has been applied conceptually but not yet stress-tested across multiple real deals.

### 2. AI-Assisted Compression Rates Adopted
**Decision:** Factor AI tool usage into labor hour estimates when calculating floor prices.
- Architecture: Traditional hours x 0.65 (35% compression)
- Core logic: Traditional hours x 0.35 (65% compression)
- UI: Traditional hours x 0.45 (55% compression)
- Testing: Traditional hours x 0.75 (25% compression)
- **Rationale:** AI tools (Cursor, Claude, Bolt AI) significantly reduce development time. Passing none of this saving to clients makes Layaa AI uncompetitive. Passing all of it destroys margins. The adopted rates balance both.
- **Impact on pricing:** Reduces floor prices, making Layaa AI more competitive while preserving healthy margins. This is a structural advantage over competitors not using AI-assisted development.

### 3. Discount Authority Tiers Established
- Up to 10%: Sales authority (Yuvaan can approve and document)
- 10-20%: Veer must approve
- Above 20%: Founders only
- Custom pricing outside tiers: Always Founders
- **Rationale:** Prevents margin erosion from ad-hoc discounting while giving sales some flexibility for competitive situations.

### 4. Product Tiering Established
- EduFlow: 3 tiers (Starter/Growth/Premium)
- CA AI Agent: 3 tiers (Solo/Practice/Firm)
- General services: 3 tiers (Starter/Growth/Enterprise)
- **Rationale:** Tiered pricing gives prospects an entry point (Starter) while creating clear upgrade paths. The anchor effect of seeing Premium pricing makes Growth look reasonable.

## INITIAL LEARNINGS

1. **The hybrid pricing formula works in theory but needs stress-testing.** First few real deals priced using Floor-Ceiling-Proposed will reveal whether the confidence multiplier ranges are calibrated correctly.
2. **EduFlow margins look healthy at current cost structure.** But this depends heavily on Shubham not spending excessive hours on implementations. Time tracking per engagement is essential.
3. **AI compression rates are a competitive moat.** Competitors who do not use AI-assisted development have 35-65% higher labor costs on the same work. This advantage should be protected — do not pass all savings to clients.
4. **Education sector is extremely price-sensitive.** The Starter tier (Rs.40K implementation) was designed specifically for this. Do not try to push Growth or Premium on budget-constrained schools.
5. **CA AI Agent pricing is theoretical.** No CA firm deals have closed. The three tiers will need validation once the product launches and real sales conversations happen. Be prepared to adjust.
6. **Scope creep is the biggest margin killer.** A Rs.2.5L deal at 40% margin drops to 25% margin (red flag) with just 20% scope expansion. Re-scoping or re-pricing must happen immediately when scope expands beyond 10%.
