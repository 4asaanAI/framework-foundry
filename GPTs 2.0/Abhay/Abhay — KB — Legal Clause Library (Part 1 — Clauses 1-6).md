# Abhay — Legal Clause Library (Part 1: Clauses 1–6)

> This is the first half of Layaa AI's standard clause library. Each clause follows this structure: Standard Language (Layaa AI default position), Negotiation Guidance, and Red Lines (non-negotiable without Founder approval).

---

## Clause 1: Scope of Work

**Standard Language:**
> The scope of services ("Services") shall be limited to those expressly described in the Statement of Work ("SoW") attached hereto as Schedule [X]. Any work not explicitly described in the SoW is excluded from this Agreement. Changes to scope require a written Change Order signed by both parties, with adjusted timelines and fees as mutually agreed.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Always define scope by reference to a separate SoW (not in the MSA body) — this allows scope changes without amending the MSA
- Include explicit exclusion language ("Services do NOT include...")
- Include a Change Order mechanism with pricing adjustment rights
- Resist "implied scope" language — if it is not written, it is not included

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept unlimited scope or "all services reasonably necessary" language
- Never accept scope expansion without corresponding fee adjustment
- Founder approval required for scope changes exceeding Rs.50,000 in incremental value

## Clause 2: IP Ownership

**Standard Language:**
> All intellectual property developed by the Service Provider ("Provider") specifically for the Client under this Agreement ("Client Deliverables") shall vest in the Client upon full payment of all fees. Pre-existing intellectual property of the Provider ("Provider IP"), including but not limited to tools, frameworks, libraries, methodologies, and reusable components, shall remain the exclusive property of the Provider. The Provider retains the right to reuse general knowledge, skills, techniques, and know-how gained during the engagement.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Layaa AI's standard position: client owns custom deliverables; Layaa AI retains all pre-existing IP, tools, and reusable components
- The "tools, frameworks, libraries" carve-out is critical — Layaa AI reuses n8n workflows, AI agent templates, and automation patterns across clients
- If the client pushes for full IP assignment, counter with a license grant for Provider IP used in the deliverables
- Never assign IP in pre-existing tools or methodologies

**Red Lines:**
- **Never assign Layaa AI's pre-existing IP** — this includes n8n workflow templates, AI agent frameworks, and reusable automation patterns
- **Work-for-hire language** must always include the Provider IP carve-out
- Any IP assignment of Provider IP requires Founder approval
- Background IP and foreground IP must always be distinguished

## Clause 3: Confidentiality

**Standard Language:**
> Each party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information received from the other party and to use such information solely for purposes of this Agreement. Confidential Information excludes: (a) information in the public domain; (b) information independently developed; (c) information received from a third party without restriction; (d) information required to be disclosed by law. This obligation survives for 3 (three) years after termination of this Agreement.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Standard survival period: 3 years post-termination. Acceptable range: 2-5 years.
- Ensure mutual confidentiality (not one-sided)
- Include carve-outs for disclosures to professional advisors and affiliates under equivalent obligations
- For trade secrets: consider "perpetual for as long as the information remains a trade secret"

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept unlimited confidentiality duration without Founder approval
- Never accept one-sided confidentiality that restricts only Layaa AI
- Never agree to confidentiality that prevents Layaa AI from disclosing the existence of the client relationship (case study rights are important for a startup)

## Clause 4: Payment Terms

**Standard Language:**
> Fees shall be as specified in the SoW. The Client shall pay 50% of the Implementation Fee upon execution of the SoW ("Advance") and the remaining 50% upon delivery and acceptance of the Deliverables. Monthly retainer fees are payable in advance by the 5th of each month. All invoices are payable within 15 (fifteen) days of receipt. Late payments attract interest at 1.5% per month. All fees are exclusive of GST (currently 18%), which shall be charged separately.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Standard split: 50/50 (advance/delivery). Acceptable: 40/60 or 30/40/30 (advance/milestone/delivery)
- Payment terms: 15 days preferred. Maximum acceptable: 30 days. Beyond 30 days requires Veer and Founder review.
- Always charge GST separately — never include in quoted fee
- Include late payment interest clause — 1.5% per month is standard
- For retainers: always payable in advance, not arrears
- For large projects (>Rs.5L): consider milestone-based payments

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept payment terms beyond 60 days — this is a Founder-level red flag
- Never accept "payment upon satisfaction" without objective acceptance criteria
- Never waive GST or absorb GST within fees
- Advance payment of at least 30% is mandatory for all new clients

## Clause 5: Limitation of Liability

**Standard Language:**
> The aggregate liability of the Provider under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable by the Client under the applicable SoW in the 12 (twelve) months preceding the claim. In no event shall the Provider be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including loss of profits, data, or business opportunity, even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Standard cap: fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim. This is the preferred position.
- Acceptable alternatives: total contract value, or 100% of fees paid under the specific SoW
- Always exclude indirect/consequential damages — this is critical for a services company
- If client pushes for higher cap, consider carve-outs (e.g., higher cap for data breach, lower cap for general liability)
- Consider including mutual limitation (applies to both parties)

**Red Lines:**
- **Never accept unlimited liability** — Founder approval required for any contract with uncapped liability
- **Never accept liability exceeding 2x contract value** without Founder approval
- Consequential damages exclusion is non-negotiable for general services liability
- Data breach liability may have a separate, higher cap — but only with Preeti's input and Founder approval

## Clause 6: Indemnification

**Standard Language:**
> The Provider shall indemnify the Client against third-party claims arising from: (a) infringement of intellectual property rights by the Deliverables; (b) gross negligence or willful misconduct by the Provider; (c) breach of confidentiality obligations. The Client shall indemnify the Provider against third-party claims arising from: (a) use of Deliverables in violation of this Agreement; (b) Client's breach of applicable laws; (c) Client-provided content or data.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Always push for mutual indemnification — not one-sided
- Indemnification should be limited to third-party claims only (direct claims are covered by liability clause)
- Include standard conditions: prompt notice, control of defense, reasonable cooperation
- IP indemnity is standard — but limit to the Deliverables, not to Provider IP used in combination with other products

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept unlimited indemnification — subject to the same cap as limitation of liability
- Never accept indemnification for the client's own negligence or misuse
- Never accept "hold harmless" language without corresponding mutual obligations
- Broad indemnification clauses require Founder review
