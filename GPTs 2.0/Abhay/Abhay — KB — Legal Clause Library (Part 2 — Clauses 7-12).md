# Abhay — Legal Clause Library (Part 2: Clauses 7–12)

> This is the second half of Layaa AI's standard clause library. Each clause follows this structure: Standard Language (Layaa AI default position), Negotiation Guidance, and Red Lines (non-negotiable without Founder approval).

---

## Clause 7: Termination

**Standard Language:**
> Either party may terminate this Agreement: (a) for convenience upon 30 (thirty) days' written notice; (b) for material breach not cured within 15 (fifteen) days of written notice; (c) immediately upon the other party's insolvency, bankruptcy, or dissolution. Upon termination: the Client shall pay for all Services rendered through the termination date; the Provider shall deliver all completed Deliverables; confidentiality and IP provisions survive termination.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Termination for convenience: 30 days is standard. Accept 15-60 days range.
- Always include payment for work done through termination date — never accept "all-or-nothing" clauses
- Include clear survival clauses (confidentiality, IP ownership, indemnification, limitation of liability)
- For retainer agreements: include a minimum commitment period (e.g., 3 months) before termination for convenience is available
- Consider including a "kill fee" for early termination of large projects (cover costs incurred)

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept termination without payment for work already performed
- Never accept termination clauses that allow client to retain deliverables without payment
- Unilateral termination by client without notice period requires Founder approval
- Never accept automatic termination triggered by subjective client dissatisfaction

## Clause 8: Data Protection

**Standard Language:**
> The Provider shall process personal data solely for purposes of performing the Services and in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and the Information Technology Act, 2000. The Provider shall implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures, notify the Client of data breaches within 72 hours of discovery, and return or securely delete Client data upon termination. Data shall be stored and processed within India unless otherwise agreed in writing.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Always reference DPDP Act 2023 and IT Act 2000 for Indian contracts
- For international clients: add GDPR reference if they have EU data subjects
- Data residency: default is India. If client requires cross-border, flag to Preeti for regulatory review.
- Breach notification: 72 hours is standard (aligned with DPDP Act requirements)
- Flag this clause to Preeti for validation on every new contract

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept liability for data breaches caused by the client's own systems or negligence
- Never accept data processing obligations that contradict Layaa AI's Privacy Policy
- Data processing agreements must be validated by Preeti before execution
- Never agree to data localization outside India without Preeti and Founder approval

## Clause 9: Force Majeure

**Standard Language:**
> Neither party shall be liable for failure or delay in performance caused by events beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, epidemics, government actions, war, strikes, failure of third-party services, or internet/telecommunications outages. The affected party must provide prompt notice and use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact. If the force majeure event continues for more than 60 (sixty) days, either party may terminate this Agreement.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Include technology-specific events (cloud service outages, API provider failures, cyber attacks)
- 60-day termination trigger is standard. Acceptable range: 30-90 days.
- Always include the mitigation obligation — it is expected under Indian contract law
- Consider including pandemic/epidemic explicitly (post-COVID best practice)

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept force majeure clauses that exclude technology failures (critical for an AI services company)
- Never accept clauses that require continued performance during force majeure events

## Clause 10: Dispute Resolution

**Standard Language:**
> The parties shall first attempt to resolve disputes through good faith negotiation for 30 (thirty) days. If unresolved, disputes shall be referred to binding arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, with a sole arbitrator mutually appointed by the parties. The seat of arbitration shall be Gurgaon, Haryana. The language of arbitration shall be English. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of India, and the courts of Gurgaon shall have exclusive jurisdiction for matters not subject to arbitration.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Arbitration is preferred over litigation for speed and confidentiality
- Sole arbitrator for disputes under Rs.50L; three-member tribunal for larger disputes
- Seat of arbitration: Gurgaon (preferred) or Delhi (acceptable)
- Always include negotiation/mediation as a first step before arbitration
- For international clients: consider SIAC (Singapore International Arbitration Centre) for cross-border disputes, but only with Founder approval

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept foreign governing law without Founder approval (Indian law is mandatory default)
- Never accept foreign jurisdiction for disputes without Founder approval
- Never accept litigation-only dispute resolution (arbitration preferred for startup)
- Never accept "client's jurisdiction" for governing law or dispute resolution

## Clause 11: Warranty / Disclaimer

**Standard Language:**
> The Provider warrants that the Services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry practices. This warranty shall apply for 30 (thirty) days following delivery ("Warranty Period"). During the Warranty Period, the Provider shall remedy any defects at no additional cost. Except as expressly stated herein, the Provider makes no other warranties, express or implied, including warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, or non-infringement.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- Warranty period: 30 days post-delivery is standard. Acceptable: 30-90 days.
- Warranty should cover defects in the Provider's work, not client-caused issues
- Include clear exclusions: changes made by client, misuse, third-party modifications, client-provided data issues
- For subscription services (maintenance): separate SLA with uptime commitments replaces warranty

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept performance guarantees (e.g., "AI will achieve X% accuracy") — AI outputs are probabilistic
- Never accept unlimited warranty periods
- Never accept warranty for third-party components (APIs, cloud services)
- ROI guarantees or specific outcome promises require Founder approval

## Clause 12: Non-Solicitation

**Standard Language:**
> During the term of this Agreement and for 12 (twelve) months following termination, neither party shall directly or indirectly solicit, hire, or engage any employee, contractor, or agent of the other party who was involved in the performance of the Services, without the prior written consent of the other party.

**Negotiation Guidance:**
- 12 months is standard. Acceptable range: 6-24 months.
- Ensure this is mutual — critical for protecting Layaa AI's AI workforce and any future human hires
- "Involved in the performance" is a key qualifier — do not agree to company-wide non-solicitation
- General job postings are typically excluded from non-solicitation

**Red Lines:**
- Never accept company-wide non-solicitation (only personnel involved in the engagement)
- Never accept non-solicitation periods beyond 24 months
- Never accept one-sided non-solicitation that binds only Layaa AI
