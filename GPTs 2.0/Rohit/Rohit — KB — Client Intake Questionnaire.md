# Rohit — KB — Client Intake Questionnaire

> Structured questionnaire for initial client conversations and industry-specific discovery notes. Use as a guide, not a rigid form.

---

## Client Intake Questionnaire

For initial conversations, use this as a guide (not a rigid form):

### Business Overview
1. Tell me about your business — what do you do and who are your customers?
2. How many people are on your team? What are the key roles?
3. What's your biggest operational challenge right now?

### Current Operations
4. Walk me through a typical day — what processes take the most time?
5. What tools and software do you currently use? (List everything, even spreadsheets)
6. Where does data currently live? How do you track things?

### Pain Points
7. If you could fix one thing about your operations tomorrow, what would it be?
8. What manual processes do you wish were automated?
9. What errors or delays happen most frequently? What do they cost you?

### Previous Attempts
10. Have you tried to automate anything before? What happened?
11. Are there tools you've evaluated but decided not to use? Why?

### Expectations & Constraints
12. What would success look like for you 90 days after we start?
13. What is your budget range for this initiative? (Note: Record, do NOT confirm pricing)
14. Are there any hard deadlines or regulatory requirements we should know about?
15. Who else needs to be involved in the decision? Who will use the final solution?

### Data & Systems
16. Do you have historical data from your current processes? How much?
17. Are your current systems accessible via API or do they only offer manual export?
18. Are there any data privacy or compliance requirements we need to know about?

## Industry-Specific Discovery Notes

### Education Sector (Active — EduFlow)
**Common pain points:** Attendance tracking, fee management, parent communication, report generation, admission processing, timetable scheduling.
**Data reality:** Often messy — paper registers being digitized, WhatsApp as primary communication.
**Key stakeholders:** Principal (decision maker), Admin staff (end users), Parents (customers).
**Regulatory:** RTE compliance, CBSE/state board requirements, fee regulation in some states.

### Professional Services — CA Firms (Active — CA AI Agent)
**Common pain points:** Document collection from clients, ITR form preparation, GST reconciliation, deadline tracking, client communication.
**Data reality:** Documents arrive via WhatsApp/email in various formats (PDF, images, scanned).
**Key stakeholders:** Senior CA (decision maker), Junior CAs/staff (end users), Clients (data providers).
**Regulatory:** Income Tax Act, GST law, ICAI guidelines. ZERO error tolerance on filings.

### SaaS Startups
**Common pain points:** Manual onboarding, support ticket routing, usage tracking, churn prediction, billing reconciliation.
**Data reality:** Usually digital-first, API-accessible systems, but often siloed across 5-10 SaaS tools.
**Key stakeholders:** Founder/CEO (decision maker + budget), Head of Ops (end user), Dev team (integration partners).
**Regulatory:** Varies by client's industry. Data privacy always relevant.

### Logistics & Supply Chain SMEs
**Common pain points:** Manual tracking, coordination between warehouse/delivery/customer, inventory management, invoice processing, compliance documentation.
**Data reality:** Mixed — some systems, some spreadsheets, some paper. Driver/field staff communication often via phone/WhatsApp.
**Key stakeholders:** Operations Manager (daily user), Owner (budget authority), Warehouse staff (end users).
**Regulatory:** GST e-waybills, transport compliance, FSSAI (if food logistics).
