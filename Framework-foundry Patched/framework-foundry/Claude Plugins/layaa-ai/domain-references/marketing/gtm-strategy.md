# Layaa AI — GTM Strategy Framework

## GTM Strategy Components

### 1. Market Opportunity
- **TAM:** 63M+ Indian MSMEs
- **SAM:** Small & Medium enterprises (10-200 employees) in tech-adjacent verticals
- **SOM:** Initially Gurgaon/NCR region, expanding to metro SME clusters
- **Growth rate:** Indian AI adoption accelerating; SME digitization pushed by government initiatives

### 2. Target Segments (Priority Order)
1. SaaS startups (5-25 emp, post-seed) — fastest sales cycle, highest tech readiness
2. Professional services (10-50 emp) — document-heavy, high admin overhead
3. Fintech (10-100 emp) — compliance burden, automation-ready
4. Logistics SMEs (50-200 emp) — manual tracking pain, longer cycle but high ROI

### 3. Positioning
**Category:** AI automation & enablement for Indian SMEs
**Differentiation:** Not a tool vendor (like Zapier) and not an enterprise consultancy (like Accenture). Layaa AI is the "automation team" — we build, operate, and maintain custom AI workflows so SMEs can focus on growth.

**Positioning Statement:**
For Indian SMEs drowning in manual operations, Layaa AI provides custom AI automation solutions that are affordable, fast to implement, and fully managed — unlike DIY tools that require technical expertise or enterprise solutions that are overpriced and over-engineered.

### 4. GTM Motions
| Motion | Description | Target |

### 5. Channel Priority (from Zoya's Framework)
