## GTM Strategy

### Market Positioning
- **Category:** AI automation & enablement for Indian SMEs
- **Differentiator:** Structured methodology + no-code delivery + ongoing partnership (not DIY tools)
- **Vs. enterprise players:** SME-specific pricing, faster delivery, more accessible
- **Vs. freelancers:** Methodology, continuity, accountability, team depth

### Channel Priority (Ranked)
1. LinkedIn (organic + paid) — where decision-makers are; highest B2B ROI
2. Content/SEO — long-term inbound; "AI for [industry] SME India" keywords
3. Google Search (paid) — high-intent; "[problem] automation India"
4. Email — nurture sequences for warm leads and retainer upsell
5. Referral/Community — founder networks, NASSCOM, industry WhatsApp groups

### LinkedIn Strategy
**Company Page (4 posts/week):**
- Monday: Week-opening insight or industry observation
- Wednesday: Case study or client win (anonymised if needed)
- Thursday: Educational tip or "how we approach [problem]"
- Friday: Team update, milestone, or behind-the-scenes

**Founder LinkedIn (3 posts/week):**
- Abhimanyu: Strategic/visionary — AI adoption trends, SME opportunity, business lessons
- Shubham: Technical/practical — workflow demos, build stories, "how we built X"

**Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday 8-10 AM IST | Saturday 9-11 AM IST

**LinkedIn Paid:** Lead gen forms for "Free AI Readiness Assessment" | Targeting: Founder/CEO/COO/Ops Director + 10-200 employees + India | Budget: ₹15,000-25,000/month | Target CPL: <₹2,000

### Messaging Pillars
1. "AI that actually works for Indian businesses" — localisation, not generic AI
2. "Operational leverage without technical teams" — no-code, accessible
3. "Results in weeks, not months" — fast implementation methodology
4. "Your automation team, not just a tool" — partnership positioning

### Content Principles
1. Lead with outcome: "Your team saves 20 hours/week" not "We built an n8n workflow"
2. Use Indian context: ₹ not $, Indian company examples, Indian regulatory references
3. Numbers > vague claims: specific figures always beat "significant savings"
4. CTA on every post: comment / DM / link / save
5. No AI hype: "automate" not "leverage cutting-edge GenAI to revolutionise"

### UTM Standard
`utm_source=[linkedin/google/email]&utm_medium=[organic/cpc/newsletter]&utm_campaign=[name]&utm_content=[variant]`

### Campaign Planning Process (Nia's Framework)
Brief intake → Audience & message selection → Channel assignment → Asset creation → QA → Launch → Monitor → Report

## Brand Voice Framework

### Core Voice Attributes
1. **Confident without arrogance** — direct statements, no excessive hedging
2. **Practical over theoretical** — real business outcomes, not AI hype
3. **Locally intelligent** — Indian business context, Indian examples, ₹ pricing
4. **Expert but accessible** — business owners, not developers
5. **Results-oriented** — what changes for the client, not what we do technically

### Tone by Channel
| Channel | Tone | Key Rule |
|---------|------|---------|
| LinkedIn Company | Authoritative, insight-led | One key insight per post, always CTA |
| LinkedIn Founder | Personal, opinionated | First-person, story or lesson, conversational |
| Email Outreach | Warm, specific | Under 150 words, one ask per email |
| Proposals | Professional, confident | Outcome-focused, specific numbers |
| Website | Clear, benefit-led | Short sentences, scannable, no jargon |

### DO / DON'T
**DO:** Indian business context | Specific numbers | Lead with client outcome | Active voice | "we" for company voice
**DON'T:** AI hype words (revolutionary/game-changing/cutting-edge) | Passive voice | "We are Layaa AI" openers | Dollar signs | Over-explain technical details | Buzzwords (synergy/ecosystem/holistic)

### Preferred Vocabulary
- "automate" not "leverage AI to streamline"
- "build" not "develop a solution"
- "Indian SMEs" not "small businesses"
- "implement" not "deploy"
- "client" not "customer" (B2B context)

### Banned Phrases
"Revolutionary AI" | "Game-changing" | "Cutting-edge" | "End-to-end ecosystem" | "Best-in-class" | "We're excited to announce" (say what you're announcing, skip the excitement)

### Signature Phrases (Approved)
- "AI that actually works for Indian businesses"
- "Scale without hiring"
- "Your automation team, not just a tool"
- "Operational leverage without technical teams"
- "Results in weeks, not months"
