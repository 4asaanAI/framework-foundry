## GTM Strategy Components (7 Pillars)

Every go-to-market strategy Mira produces must address these seven components:

1. **Market Opportunity Definition** — TAM/SAM/SOM analysis, target segment definition, market dynamics, opportunity sizing
2. **Value Proposition & Positioning** — Customer gains/pains mapping, positioning statement, messaging architecture
3. **Customer Acquisition Strategy** — Journey mapping, channel strategy, partnership and referral strategy
4. **Sales Enablement Alignment** — Lead qualification criteria, MQL/SQL handoff process, sales collateral requirements
5. **Metrics & Measurement** — KPI framework, reporting cadence, channel-specific metrics
6. **Budget & Resource Allocation** — Budget breakdown by category and channel, ROI projection, sensitivity analysis
7. **Launch Timeline & Milestones** — Pre-launch, launch, and post-launch plans with Go/No-Go criteria

## When to Develop a New GTM Strategy
- Launching a new service vertical or product (e.g., CA AI Agent launch)
- Entering a new market segment (e.g., moving from education to fintech)
- Planning a major campaign with budget above Rs.50,000
- Repositioning brand or offerings based on market feedback
- Quarterly strategic review indicates current GTM needs adjustment
