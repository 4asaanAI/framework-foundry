## INC-20A — Declaration of Commencement of Business

**Purpose:** Declares that the company has commenced business operations and that every subscriber has paid the value of shares agreed to be taken.

**Deadline:** Within 180 days of incorporation. For Layaa AI (incorporated 19 Dec 2024): **by approximately 17 June 2025.**

**Prerequisites:**
- All subscribers must have paid the value of shares as stated in the MOA
- Company must have filed a verification of its registered office (INC-22)

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] Bank statement showing receipt of share subscription money from each subscriber
- [ ] Proof of registered office filing (INC-22 confirmation)
- [ ] Board resolution authorizing the filing
- [ ] DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) of a director

**Filing Fee:** Rs.500 (GNL-2 form filing fee as per MCA fee schedule for authorized capital up to Rs.1 Lakh)

**Penalty for Non-Filing:** Rs.50,000 on the company AND each officer in default. Additionally, the Registrar may initiate action to remove the company name (strike-off). CIN can be flagged as "ACTIVE-non-compliant."

**Practical Notes:**
- This is a one-time filing. Once done, never needs to be filed again.
- The bank statement is the critical document — ensure share subscription money is clearly reflected in the company's current account.
- If subscribers paid in cash, that creates issues — bank transfer is strongly recommended.
- Coordinate with Aarav to ensure bank statement is available showing the subscription amount.

## ADT-1 — Auditor Appointment

**Purpose:** Intimation to the Registrar about the appointment of the statutory auditor.

**Deadline:** Within 15 days of the AGM at which the auditor was appointed. For the first auditor appointed by the Board (within 30 days of incorporation), ADT-1 must be filed within 15 days of the Board meeting.

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] Board resolution / shareholders' resolution appointing the auditor
- [ ] Written consent of the auditor (signed letter)
- [ ] Auditor's certificate confirming eligibility under Section 141 (not disqualified)
- [ ] Auditor's membership number (ICAI)
- [ ] Auditor's firm registration number (if a firm)
- [ ] DSC of the authorized signatory (director)

**Board Resolution Format (Template):**
> "RESOLVED THAT pursuant to Section 139 of the Companies Act, 2013, [Auditor Name / Firm Name], Chartered Accountants (Membership No. / FRN: [number]), be and are hereby appointed as the Statutory Auditor of the Company to hold office from the conclusion of this meeting until the conclusion of the [Nth] Annual General Meeting, at a remuneration of Rs.[amount] plus applicable GST and out-of-pocket expenses."

**Filing Fee:** Rs.300 (for authorized capital up to Rs.1 Lakh)

**Penalty:** Rs.300 per day of delay, on the company and every officer in default.

**Practical Notes:**
- The auditor must confirm they have not been disqualified under Section 141 (convicted of offence, indebted to the company, related party issues, etc.)
- First auditor appointment: Board can appoint within 30 days of incorporation. This auditor holds office until the first AGM. At the first AGM, shareholders must ratify or appoint a new auditor for a 5-year term.
- Rotation: For private companies with paid-up capital below Rs.50 Crore, auditor rotation provisions do not apply. Layaa AI can retain the same auditor.

## AOC-4 / AOC-4 CFS — Financial Statements Filing

**Purpose:** Filing of annual financial statements with the Registrar.

**AOC-4:** For standalone financial statements (used by Layaa AI — no subsidiaries).
**AOC-4 CFS:** For consolidated financial statements (not applicable to Layaa AI currently).

**Deadline:** Within 30 days of the AGM.

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] Audited Balance Sheet (signed by both directors + auditor)
- [ ] Audited Statement of Profit & Loss
- [ ] Cash Flow Statement
- [ ] Statement of Changes in Equity (if applicable)
- [ ] Notes to Financial Statements
- [ ] Independent Auditor's Report
- [ ] Board's Report (signed by both directors)
- [ ] Directors' Responsibility Statement (part of Board's Report)
- [ ] DSC of director, company secretary (if applicable), CFO (if applicable)
- [ ] Notice of AGM
- [ ] Copy of AGM resolution adopting accounts

**XBRL Filing:** Not applicable to Layaa AI. XBRL filing is mandatory for companies with paid-up capital of Rs.5 Crore+ or turnover of Rs.100 Crore+. Layaa AI files in non-XBRL (HTML/PDF) format.

**Filing Fee:** Based on authorized capital. For Layaa AI (authorized capital Rs.1 Lakh): Rs.200.

**Penalty:** Rs.100 per day of delay (on the company and every officer in default), maximum Rs.10 Lakh.

**Practical Notes:**
- The Board's Report is a critical document that Anne should ensure is drafted. It must include: state of company's affairs, amounts proposed as dividends (if any), material changes since FY end, directors' responsibility statement, and details of Board meetings held.
- For the first financial year (which may be a short year or extended year depending on incorporation date), ensure the CA prepares statements for the correct period.
- Layaa AI's first FY: 19 Dec 2024 to 31 March 2025 (approximately 3.5 months). Financial statements will cover this short period.

## MGT-7 / MGT-7A — Annual Return

**Purpose:** Filing of the annual return containing company details, shareholders, directors, meetings held, and other prescribed information.

**MGT-7:** Full annual return (for companies with paid-up capital Rs.10 Crore+ or turnover Rs.50 Crore+).
**MGT-7A:** Abridged annual return for small companies and one-person companies. **Layaa AI files MGT-7A** (qualifies as small company — paid-up capital and turnover both below thresholds).

**Deadline:** Within 60 days of the AGM.

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] Registered office address and details
- [ ] Principal business activities with NIC code (62099 for Layaa AI)
- [ ] Details of holding/subsidiary companies (N/A)
- [ ] Share capital structure and shareholding pattern
- [ ] Membership and debenture details (list of shareholders with shares held)
- [ ] Particulars of directors and KMP (Key Managerial Personnel)
- [ ] Details of all Board Meetings and General Meetings held during the year
- [ ] Remuneration paid to directors and KMP
- [ ] Details of penalties/punishments/compounding (if any)
- [ ] Certification by a Company Secretary in practice (for MGT-7; for MGT-7A, director can sign)
- [ ] DSC of director

**Filing Fee:** Rs.200 (for authorized capital up to Rs.1 Lakh)

**Penalty:** Rs.100 per day of delay (maximum Rs.5 Lakh), on company and every officer in default.

**Practical Notes:**
- MGT-7A is simpler than MGT-7 but still requires accurate data about shareholding, meetings, and directors.
- Ensure all Board Meeting minutes are up to date before filing — the return requires dates and attendance details of each Board Meeting.
- Cross-check shareholding pattern with the Register of Members.
- If Layaa AI's turnover crosses Rs.2 Crore or paid-up capital crosses Rs.4 Crore (small company thresholds), it must file MGT-7 (full return) instead of MGT-7A.

## DIR-3 KYC — Director KYC

**Purpose:** Annual KYC verification for all directors holding a valid DIN (Director Identification Number).

**Deadline:** 30 September each year. Both directors of Layaa AI must file individually.

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] Director's PAN card
- [ ] Director's Aadhaar card (optional but strongly recommended for OTP verification)
- [ ] Current residential address proof (passport / voter ID / driving license / utility bill not older than 2 months)
- [ ] Director's personal mobile number (for OTP)
- [ ] Director's personal email address (for OTP)
- [ ] DSC of the director
- [ ] Passport-size photograph (if filing for the first time or details changed)

**Process:**
1. If filing for the first time after DIN allotment: File DIR-3 KYC form with all documents + CA certification
2. Subsequent years (no change in details): File DIR-3 KYC-WEB (simplified online verification via OTP on mobile + email)
3. If details have changed (address, mobile, email): File full DIR-3 KYC form again with updated documents

**Filing Fee:** NIL if filed on time. **Rs.5,000 penalty per director** if filed after 30 September.

**Penalty Impact:** DIN gets deactivated if KYC not filed. Deactivated DIN means the director cannot sign any MCA form, which blocks ALL other company filings. This cascades — a missed DIR-3 KYC can block AOC-4, MGT-7, and everything else.

**Practical Notes:**
- Set reminders for August (30 days before deadline). Both Abhimanyu and Shubham must complete this individually.
- The most common issue: OTP not received on mobile/email. Ensure mobile numbers and email addresses registered with MCA are current and active.
- If a director's DIN gets deactivated, reactivation requires filing DIR-3 KYC with the Rs.5,000 fee and waiting for MCA processing (can take 2-4 weeks).

## DIR-8 — Director Disclosure

**Purpose:** Annual disclosure by each director about their interests in other entities, disqualifications, and other prescribed matters under Section 184(1) and Section 164(2).

**When:** At the first Board Meeting of each financial year (typically April/May Board Meeting).

**What Each Director Discloses:**
- Directorships held in other companies
- Memberships in other bodies corporate
- Shareholding in other companies (if material)
- Any disqualification under Section 164 (insolvency, conviction, non-filing defaults)
- Interests in contracts/arrangements with the company

**Filed With:** Not filed with MCA. Kept in the company's Board Meeting records (minutes + signed DIR-8 form as annexure). However, the disclosure must be noted in Board Meeting minutes.

**Practical Notes:**
- Anne should remind both directors before the first Board Meeting of each FY to prepare their DIR-8 disclosures.
- If either director takes up a directorship in another company during the year, they must disclose at the next Board Meeting (not just annually).

## DPT-3 — Return of Deposits

**Purpose:** Annual return of deposits and transactions not considered as deposits, filed with the Registrar.

**Deadline:** 30 June each year (for information as of 31 March).

**Applicability for Layaa AI:**
- If Layaa AI has received any loan from shareholders, directors, or third parties, DPT-3 may be required even if the amounts are not technically "deposits" under the Companies Act.
- Loans from directors (if taken) are exempt from deposit rules but must still be reported in DPT-3.
- If Layaa AI has no deposits and no outstanding loans from directors/shareholders, verify with CA whether NIL filing is required.

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] Details of all deposits accepted/outstanding as of 31 March
- [ ] Details of transactions not considered as deposits (director loans, inter-corporate deposits)
- [ ] Auditor's certificate (if deposits are outstanding)
- [ ] DSC of authorized signatory

**Filing Fee:** Rs.200 (for authorized capital up to Rs.1 Lakh)

**Penalty:** Rs.5,000 + Rs.500 per day of continuing default.

## INC-22A — Active Company Tagging (ACTIVE)

**Purpose:** MCA verification that the company is active and has a functional registered office.

**Current Status:** INC-22A was introduced via the ACTIVE scheme (Active Company Tagging Identities and Verification). Companies that did not file were tagged as "ACTIVE non-compliant." Check if this is applicable to Layaa AI based on the current MCA notification timeline.

**Requirements:**
- Photograph of registered office (showing external building and inside office)
- Latitude and longitude of registered office
- Details of statutory records maintained at registered office

**Practical Notes:**

## Form BEN-2 — Declaration of Significant Beneficial Ownership

**Purpose:** Company's return to the Registrar about individuals who hold significant beneficial ownership (SBO) in the company.

**When Required:** Within 30 days of receiving a BEN-1 declaration from a member (shareholder who is not the beneficial owner, or who holds shares on behalf of someone else).

**Applicability for Layaa AI:**
- If Abhimanyu and Shubham hold shares directly in their own names (not through trusts, HUFs, partnerships, or holding companies), BEN-2 is likely not required as there is no separation between registered and beneficial ownership.
- If any future investor holds shares through an entity (trust, company, LLP), BEN-1 and BEN-2 filings become mandatory.

**Documents Required:**
- [ ] BEN-1 declaration received from the member
- [ ] Details of the significant beneficial owner (name, address, PAN, nationality, shares held)
- [ ] DSC of authorized signatory

**Penalty for Non-Filing:** Rs.1 Lakh on the company + Rs.10 Lakh if default continues.

**Practical Notes:**
- Current exposure is minimal (2 individual shareholders). Track this when fundraising — any investor holding shares through a corporate entity triggers BEN-1/BEN-2 requirements.
- Threshold for SBO: Holding 10%+ shares or voting rights, or right to receive 10%+ of distributable dividend, or exercising significant influence or control.
