each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by either
Party, whether oral, written, electronic, or visual, including but
not limited to:
(a) Business plans, strategies, financial data, pricing models
(b) Client lists, vendor details, employee information
(c) Technical data, software code, algorithms, AI models, prompts
(d) Product roadmaps, designs, prototypes, trade secrets
(e) Any information marked "Confidential" or that a reasonable person
    would understand to be confidential given the nature and context

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. OBLIGATIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.1 The receiving Party shall: (a) maintain strict confidentiality,
    (b) use Confidential Information solely for the stated Purpose,
    (c) restrict disclosure to employees/contractors who need to know
    and are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.
3.2 The receiving Party shall protect Confidential Information with at
    least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential
    information, but no less than reasonable care.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. EXCLUSIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Confidential Information does not include information that:
(a) Was publicly available at the time of disclosure or becomes so
    through no breach of this NDA
(b) Was already in the receiving Party's possession without restriction
(c) Is independently developed by the receiving Party
(d) Is received from a third party without confidentiality obligation
(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order
    (provided the disclosing Party is notified promptly)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. TERM
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5.1 This NDA is effective from the date of execution and shall remain
    in force for a period of 2 (two) years from the Effective Date.
5.2 Confidentiality obligations shall survive the expiry or termination
    of this NDA for a further period of 2 (two) years.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. RETURN / DESTRUCTION OF INFORMATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6.1 Upon written request or expiry of this NDA, the receiving Party
    shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information and
    certify the same in writing within 15 days.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. NO LICENSE OR OBLIGATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7.1 Nothing in this NDA grants any license, intellectual property
    rights, or obligation to enter into any further agreement.
7.2 Neither Party is obligated to disclose any particular information.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. REMEDIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8.1 The Parties acknowledge that breach of this NDA may cause
    irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be insufficient.
    The disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief
    in addition to any other remedies available at law.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9.1 This NDA shall be governed by the laws of India, specifically the
    Indian Contract Act, 1872 and the Information Technology Act, 2000.
9.2 The courts of Gurgaon, Haryana shall have exclusive jurisdiction.

## Usage Notes for Abhay

1. **Document numbering**: Follow format `LAI/[TYPE]/[FY]/[SEQ]` — e.g., `LAI/MSA/2526/001` for FY 2025-26.
2. **Jurisdiction default**: Always Gurgaon, Haryana unless client negotiates an alternative.
3. **Governing law**: Indian Contract Act, 1872 and IT Act, 2000 are the primary statutes. Reference the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 for dispute resolution.
4. **Payment default**: 50% advance / 50% delivery. This can be modified per SoW but should be documented explicitly.
5. **NDA term**: Default 2 years for the NDA itself, plus 2 years survival for confidentiality obligations.
6. **IP position**: Layaa AI retains core IP (EduFlow, CA AI Agent, internal tools). Clients get a non-exclusive license to custom Deliverables upon full payment.
7. **All legal documents** should be reviewed by a qualified legal professional before execution. These templates provide a strong starting point but are not a substitute for legal counsel.
8. **E-signatures**: These agreements can be executed via electronic signature platforms (DocuSign, Digio) in compliance with the Information Technology Act, 2000, Section 5.
