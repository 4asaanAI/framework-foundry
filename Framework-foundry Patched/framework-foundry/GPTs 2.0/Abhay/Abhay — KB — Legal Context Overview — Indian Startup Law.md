## DPIIT Startup Recognition (DIPP245808)

- **Benefits:** Self-certification for 9 labour and 3 environmental laws, tax exemption eligibility under Section 80-IAC, fast-track patent applications (80% rebate on patent fees), easier public procurement norms, SIDBI Fund of Funds access
- **Tax exemption (Section 80-IAC):** 3 consecutive years of tax holiday out of first 10 years from incorporation. Requires Inter-Ministerial Board certification. Turnover must not exceed Rs.100 Cr in any financial year.
- **Angel Tax Exemption (Section 56(2)(viib)):** DPIIT-recognized startups are exempt from angel tax on share premium. Requires DPIIT certificate and Form 2 filing.
- **Valid until:** December 2035

## Udyam MSME Registration (UDYAM-HR-05-0177880)

- **Category:** Micro Enterprise (Services sector)
- **Benefits:** Priority sector lending, collateral-free loans (CGTMSE), 25% subsidy on patent/trademark filing, government tender preference, delayed payment protection (45-day rule under MSMED Act)
- **Classification thresholds:** Micro (Investment up to Rs.1 Cr, Turnover up to Rs.5 Cr)

---

## Trademark (Class 42)

- **Status:** Filed, pending registration
- **Class 42:** Scientific and technological services, computer software design, SaaS
- **Protection:** Use TM symbol until registration is granted; switch to (R) symbol after registration
- **Monitoring:** Watch for similar marks in Class 42 and Class 35 (advertising/business services)

---

## ESOPs and Equity Framework

- **Companies Act 2013 compliance:** Section 62(1)(b) — ESOP requires special resolution, 1-year minimum vesting period
- **DPIIT startups:** Sweat equity provisions under Section 54 — eligible for up to 25% of paid-up share capital
- **Tax implications for employees:** Taxed as perquisite at exercise (FMV - exercise price) under Section 17(2)
- **Current status:** No ESOPs issued. Any ESOP scheme requires board resolution + special resolution + valuation report.
