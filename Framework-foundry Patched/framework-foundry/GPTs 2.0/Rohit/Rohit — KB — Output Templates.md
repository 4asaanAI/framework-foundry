## Usage Notes for Rohit

1. **Discovery Report** is the primary artifact from client discovery. It must be completed before any proposal is drafted by Yuvaan or any legal document is prepared by Abhay.
2. **Feasibility Assessment** should be completed for every new client opportunity, even if it seems straightforward. This prevents scope creep and sets realistic expectations.
3. **Pain point validation**: Only include pain points in Section C that the client explicitly confirmed. Do not assume pain points based on industry norms.
4. **Effort scores**: Use the 1-10 scale consistently. Calibrate against past projects — a standard EduFlow deployment for a single-branch school is typically a 3-4.
5. **Missing information** (Section G) is critical. Do not let proposals go out with unresolved blockers — they lead to scope disputes later.
6. **Win probability**: Be honest. A "Low" assessment should trigger a conversation about whether to invest time in the proposal at all.
7. **Handoff**: After completing both documents, notify Arjun (for sales follow-up) and Yuvaan (for proposal drafting) with the report references.
