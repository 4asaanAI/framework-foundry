# Arjun — KB — Opportunity Scoring Matrix

> Weighted scoring system for evaluating potential client opportunities, with interpretation guidelines for prioritization.

---

## Scoring Dimensions

Score each potential opportunity on these dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | 1 (Low) | 3 (Medium) | 5 (High) |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------|----------|
| Pain Severity | 25% | Nice-to-have | Significant frustration | Critical business impact |
| Budget Fit | 20% | Below minimum viable | Standard range | Premium range |
| Decision Readiness | 15% | Exploring | Evaluating options | Ready to commit |
| Technical Feasibility | 15% | Likely complex | Standard complexity | Straightforward |
| Strategic Value | 15% | One-off project | Repeat potential | Strategic account + referral |
| Timeline Urgency | 10% | No deadline | Moderate urgency | Urgent need |

---

## How to Calculate

1. Score each dimension (1, 2, 3, 4, or 5)
2. Multiply each score by its weight
3. Sum all weighted scores
4. Result is on a 1.0-5.0 scale

**Example Calculation:**
| Dimension | Weight | Score | Weighted |
|-----------|--------|-------|----------|
| Pain Severity | 25% | 4 | 1.00 |
| Budget Fit | 20% | 3 | 0.60 |
| Decision Readiness | 15% | 5 | 0.75 |
| Technical Feasibility | 15% | 4 | 0.60 |
| Strategic Value | 15% | 3 | 0.45 |
| Timeline Urgency | 10% | 4 | 0.40 |
| **Total** | | | **3.80** |

---

## Score Interpretation

- **4.0-5.0: High priority** — Fast-track discovery and proposal. These are ideal fits with high pain, budget, and readiness. Allocate senior attention. Target: proposal within 1 week of discovery.

- **3.0-3.9: Medium priority** — Proceed with standard timeline. Good potential but may need nurturing on one or more dimensions. Target: proposal within 2 weeks of discovery.

- **2.0-2.9: Low priority** — Nurture, don't rush. The opportunity exists but timing or fit isn't right. Keep in pipeline with periodic touchpoints. Revisit quarterly.

- **Below 2.0: Not a fit right now** — Archive with reason. Document why (budget too low, no real pain, not ready to decide). May revisit if circumstances change.

## Scoring Guidelines by Dimension

### Pain Severity (25%)
- **Score 1:** "It would be nice" — no quantifiable impact
- **Score 2:** Minor annoyance, workaround exists and is acceptable
- **Score 3:** Significant time drain, regular frustration, measurable cost
- **Score 4:** Major operational bottleneck, causing business loss
- **Score 5:** Critical — business cannot scale or sustain without fixing this

### Budget Fit (20%)
- **Score 1:** Below Rs.50K implementation — below minimum viable engagement
- **Score 2:** Rs.50K-1L — tight, possible only for quick wins
- **Score 3:** Rs.1-3L — standard range for most engagements
- **Score 4:** Rs.3-5L — comfortable, allows for comprehensive solution
- **Score 5:** Rs.5L+ — premium engagement with room for expansion

### Decision Readiness (15%)
- **Score 1:** Just exploring, no timeline, no budget allocated
- **Score 2:** Interested but needs internal alignment
- **Score 3:** Evaluating options, budget tentatively allocated
- **Score 4:** Active evaluation, comparing vendors
- **Score 5:** Ready to commit, needs proposal to proceed

### Technical Feasibility (15%)
- **Score 1:** Novel requirements, no clear path with current tools
- **Score 2:** Possible but requires significant custom development
- **Score 3:** Standard complexity, familiar integration patterns
- **Score 4:** Well-understood problem, proven solutions exist
- **Score 5:** Straightforward, can reuse existing patterns/templates

### Strategic Value (15%)
- **Score 1:** One-off project, no expansion potential, no referral value
- **Score 2:** Single project but in a good industry
- **Score 3:** Repeat potential or good referral network
- **Score 4:** Strategic industry + expansion + referral potential
- **Score 5:** Anchor account potential — could become showcase client

### Timeline Urgency (10%)
- **Score 1:** No deadline, "sometime this year"
- **Score 2:** Loose deadline, "next quarter"
- **Score 3:** Moderate, "next 2-3 months"
- **Score 4:** Pressing, "next month"
- **Score 5:** Urgent, "this week/next week" — external deadline driving urgency
