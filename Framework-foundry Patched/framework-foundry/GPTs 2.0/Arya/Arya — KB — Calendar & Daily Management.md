## Meeting Prep Checklist

Use this for every external meeting. Start prep at least 2 hours before meeting time.

```
## Meeting Prep: [Meeting Title] — [Date, Time]

### 1. Research the Attendee(s)
- [ ] LinkedIn profile reviewed — recent posts, role, company
- [ ] Past interactions with Layaa AI (check memory, emails, CRM)
- [ ] Their company context: size, industry, recent news
- [ ] Mutual connections or shared context (DTU alumni, common contacts)

### 2. Prepare the Agenda
- [ ] Primary objective: What does Abhimanyu want from this meeting?
- [ ] Secondary objective: What's the stretch outcome?
- [ ] 3 talking points prepared with supporting data
- [ ] Relevant materials ready (deck, proposal, demo, case study)

### 3. Key Questions to Ask
- [ ] [Discovery question — understand their situation]
- [ ] [Qualification question — assess fit/budget/timeline]
- [ ] [Closing question — what's the next step?]

### 4. Desired Outcome
- [ ] Clear: What does success look like when this meeting ends?
- [ ] Documented: Write it down so it's measurable post-meeting

### 5. Logistics
- [ ] Calendar invite sent with agenda
- [ ] Video link or location confirmed
- [ ] Backup contact method if tech fails (phone number)
- [ ] Relevant documents shared in advance (if applicable)
```

## Energy Management Principles

Abhimanyu's energy is a finite resource. Manage it like a budget.

1. **High-energy tasks in the morning:** Strategy, writing, big decisions, creative work. This is when judgment is sharpest.
2. **Relationship tasks in the afternoon:** Meetings and calls require social energy but not peak cognitive energy. Afternoon is ideal.
3. **Admin in the late afternoon:** Email, delegation, routine approvals. Low cognitive demand. Appropriate for lower-energy hours.
4. **Protect recovery time:** Lunch is a break, not a working lunch. Post-6 PM is personal. Sunday is off. Burnout is the biggest risk for a 2-person startup.
5. **Context switching costs:** Every time Abhimanyu switches from one type of work to another, there's a 10-15 minute ramp-up cost. Batch similar tasks together. Don't schedule a client call between two deep work blocks.
6. **Decision fatigue is real:** Front-load important decisions to the morning. Defer non-urgent decisions to the next day rather than making them when depleted.
7. **Movement breaks:** Encourage a 5-minute walk between meetings. Physical movement resets mental energy.

## Scheduling Rules for Arya

When scheduling on Abhimanyu's behalf:

- **Never schedule meetings before 11:00 AM** unless Abhimanyu explicitly approves
- **Never schedule back-to-back meetings** without at least a 15-minute buffer
- **Always include an agenda** in the calendar invite — no agendas, no meetings
- **Default meeting length is 30 minutes.** Only extend to 60 if the agenda genuinely requires it.
- **Fridays after 3:00 PM** — no external meetings. This is review and planning time.
- **If someone proposes a meeting that could be an email** — suggest an email instead, politely
- **For recurring meetings** — review monthly: is this still necessary?
- **Travel buffer** — if a meeting is in-person, block 30 minutes before and after for commute
