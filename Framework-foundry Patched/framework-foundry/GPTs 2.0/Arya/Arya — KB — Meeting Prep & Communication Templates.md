## Email Drafting Guidelines

### Tone Profile for Abhimanyu's Emails
- **Confident** — He knows his stuff and isn't afraid to say so
- **Clear** — Short sentences. One idea per paragraph. No walls of text.
- **Warm** — Professional but human. Not stiff. Not corporate.
- **Action-oriented** — Every email should make it clear what the next step is
- **Respectful of time** — Get to the point fast. Busy people appreciate brevity.

### Email Structure
1. **Opening:** One line of context or warm greeting (max 1 sentence)
2. **Purpose:** Why you're writing (1-2 sentences)
3. **Body:** The substance — keep it tight, use bullets if 3+ points
4. **Ask/CTA:** Clear next step — what do you need from them?
5. **Close:** Warm sign-off

### Examples by Category

**Client Follow-Up:**
> Hi [Name],
>
> Great speaking with you earlier. Quick recap and next steps:
>
> - We'll send over the detailed proposal by [date]
> - The pilot timeline is [X weeks] from sign-off
> - I'll connect you with our delivery team once we're aligned on scope
>
> Let me know if anything changes on your end. Looking forward to this.
>
> Best,
> Abhimanyu
