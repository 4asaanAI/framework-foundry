ARTICLE 4: VESTING SCHEDULE
4.1 Vesting Period
The Shares held by each Shareholder shall vest over a period of four (4) years from the date of this Agreement (the "Vesting Commencement Date"), subject to the Shareholder's continued involvement with the Company, as follows:
Cliff Period: No Shares shall vest during the first twelve (12) months from the Vesting Commencement Date (the "Cliff Period");
Cliff Vesting: Upon completion of the Cliff Period, twenty-five percent (25%) of the total Shares allocated to each Shareholder shall vest;
Monthly Vesting: Following the Cliff Period, the remaining seventy-five percent (75%) of Shares shall vest in equal monthly installments over the subsequent thirty-six (36) months.
4.2 Vesting Schedule Table
For illustrative purposes, the vesting schedule is as follows:
Month 0-12: 0% vested (Cliff Period)
Month 12: 25% vested (Cliff Vesting)
Month 13-48: Monthly vesting of 2.0833% (75% ÷ 36 months)
Month 48: 100% fully vested
4.3 Acceleration of Vesting
Vesting may be accelerated in the following circumstances:
Change of Control: Upon a sale of the Company or merger resulting in change of control, all Unvested Shares shall immediately vest;
Mutual Agreement: With unanimous written consent of all Shareholders;
Death or Permanent Incapacity: All Unvested Shares shall immediately vest in the event of a Shareholder's death or permanent incapacity as certified by a qualified medical practitioner.
4.4 Treatment of Unvested Shares Upon Exit
Upon a Shareholder ceasing involvement with the Company:
Good Leaver: Retains all Vested Shares; Unvested Shares shall be bought back by the Company at Fair Market Value or cancelled at the Company's discretion;
Bad Leaver: All Unvested Shares shall immediately be forfeited and transferred back to the Company for nominal consideration of ₹1 per share. Vested Shares may be purchased by the Company or remaining Shareholders at the lower of (a) Fair Market Value or (b) the original subscription price.

ARTICLE 6: MANAGEMENT AND GOVERNANCE
6.1 Board of Directors
The Company shall be managed by a Board of Directors consisting of two (2) directors. Each Shareholder shall be entitled to nominate one (1) director to the Board. A director nominated by a Shareholder shall serve so long as the nominating Shareholder holds Shares in the Company and may not be removed without that Shareholder’s prior written consent, except for cause (including fraud, gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or criminal conviction).
Managing Director Authority. Abhimanyu Singh (Shareholder 1) shall serve as Managing Director and CEO of the Company. In this capacity, he shall have authority to manage the Company’s day-to-day operations, execute routine contracts below the thresholds in Article 6.2, make operational decisions consistent with the approved business plan and budget, and represent the Company in client engagements and external affairs, without requiring prior Board approval for each individual action. This authority is subject at all times to the Reserved Matters provisions in Article 6.2 and the good faith obligations in Article 6.5.
Board Deadlock on Non-Reserved Matters. Where both directors cannot agree on a matter that is neither a Reserved Matter nor subject to shareholder approval, and such disagreement persists for fifteen (15) days after written notice of deadlock, the matter shall be put to a shareholder vote in which each Shareholder votes in proportion to their shareholding percentage. This mechanism reflects the 60-40 ownership split and prevents operational paralysis on routine matters whilst preserving the minority’s voice at the Board level.
6.2 Reserved Matters
Reserved Matters are the single, authoritative source of governance thresholds for this Company. All other clauses in this Agreement that reference approval thresholds or shareholder consent shall be read as cross-references to this Article, and in the event of any conflict between another clause and this Article 6.2, this Article shall prevail. Reserved Matters are divided into two categories:
Category A — Majority-Decidable Matters (require prior written approval of Shareholders holding more than fifty percent (50%) of the issued share capital, subject to the consultation process in Article 6.3). The Majority Shareholder retains ultimate authority on these matters after following the required process:
Incurring borrowings, debt, or financial obligations exceeding ₹5,00,000 in aggregate;
Entering into any agreement, contract, or commitment exceeding ₹3,00,000 per annum;
Entering into transactions with related parties above ₹1,00,000;
Appointment or removal of statutory auditors;
Creation of any subsidiary or joint venture;
Any material operational pivot or new business line within the Company’s existing business scope.
Category B — Unanimous Matters (require the unanimous written consent of all Shareholders, irrespective of shareholding percentage). These are the Minority Shareholder’s core, non-waivable protections. No action may be taken on any Category B matter without both Shareholders’ express written consent, and no resolution, board decision, or exercise of majority authority shall override this requirement:
Any material change in the business scope of the Company or entry into a substantially different line of business (consistent with Article 2.2);
Any issuance, allotment, or transfer of Shares or securities that would dilute any Shareholder’s percentage ownership below their current holding (consistent with Article 3.2);
Any acquisition, merger, or sale of the Company or all or substantially all of its assets or business (consistent with Articles 14.1 and 14.2);
Any amendment to the Articles of Association or Memorandum of Association that materially affects the rights or economic entitlements of any Shareholder;
Voluntary winding up, liquidation, or dissolution of the Company;
Sale, lease, licence, or disposal of Company IP or core technology assets that would materially impair the Company’s business;
Any amendment to the rights, obligations, profit entitlement, or equity percentage of an individual Shareholder under this Agreement;
Removal of a Shareholder from their defined founder role or responsibilities as set out in Article 5, without cause.
6.3 Consultation and Objection Process (Category A Matters)
Before the Majority Shareholder proceeds with any Category A Reserved Matter, the following process is mandatory. This process does not remove the Majority Shareholder’s right to ultimately decide Category A matters; it ensures the Minority Shareholder has a genuine, structured, and documented opportunity to be heard:
Written Notice: The proposing Shareholder shall give written notice to the other Shareholder describing the proposed action, its rationale, relevant financial information, and proposed timeline.
Objection Window — 15 days: The other Shareholder shall have fifteen (15) days from receipt to deliver a written objection with reasons and any alternative proposals. Silence within this window shall be treated as no objection and the Majority Shareholder may proceed.
Good-Faith Discussion — 15 days: If an objection is received, both Shareholders shall meet and discuss in good faith for a further fifteen (15) days to seek an agreed outcome. These discussions are confidential and without prejudice.
Majority Decision with Mandatory Documentation: If no agreement is reached after the discussion period, the Majority Shareholder may proceed with the Category A matter. The Majority Shareholder must document in the board minutes: (a) a summary of the objections raised; (b) the steps taken in good faith to address them; and (c) the rationale for proceeding. Failure to comply with this documentation requirement shall render the action voidable at the Minority Shareholder’s election within thirty (30) days of discovering the non-compliance.
6.4 Minority Shareholder Information Rights
The Minority Shareholder’s ability to exercise their rights under this Agreement depends on access to timely and accurate information. The Company shall therefore provide the following to all Shareholders on an ongoing basis, regardless of their shareholding percentage:
Monthly financial summaries (P&L, revenue, and expense reports) within fifteen (15) days of each month-end;
Access to inspect the Company’s books, accounts, and material contracts upon seven (7) days’ written notice;
Advance notice of all Board meetings and the right to attend and be heard in their capacity as Shareholder;
Copies of all board minutes within seven (7) days of each Board meeting;
Prior written notification of any Category A Reserved Matter before any action is taken, in accordance with Article 6.3.
6.5 Good Faith Duty
All Shareholders and directors shall act reasonably and in good faith in exercising their rights and authorities under this Agreement. The Majority Shareholder shall not exercise any approval right or majority authority in a manner designed solely to harm the economic or governance position of the Minority Shareholder, or to circumvent that Shareholder’s Category B protections. Any exercise of majority authority that is shown to have been made in bad faith, for an improper purpose, or in breach of the process requirements of this Article shall be voidable at the Minority Shareholder’s election within thirty (30) days of discovery.
6.6 Board Meetings
The Board shall meet at least once every quarter. Meetings may be held in person or via video/audio conference. Notice of at least seven (7) days shall be provided for regular meetings, or such shorter period as both directors agree in writing. Board decisions are made by simple majority of directors present and entitled to vote, subject always to the Reserved Matters provisions in Article 6.2 and the Managing Director authority in Article 6.1.

ARTICLE 13: TRANSFER OF SHARES
13.1 General Restriction
No Shareholder shall sell, transfer, assign, pledge, hypothecate, or otherwise dispose of any Shares (or any interest therein) except in accordance with the provisions of this Article and the Act.
13.2 Right of First Refusal (ROFR)
If a Shareholder ("Selling Shareholder") wishes to transfer any Shares, the following procedure shall apply:
Notice: The Selling Shareholder shall provide written notice to all other Shareholders and the Company, specifying the number of Shares to be sold, the proposed purchase price, and the identity of the proposed purchaser;
Offer Period: The other Shareholders shall have thirty (30) days from receipt of the notice to elect to purchase the offered Shares on the same terms, pro-rata to their existing shareholding;
Acceptance: If the other Shareholders elect to purchase all offered Shares, the transaction shall be completed within sixty (60) days;
Third Party Sale: If the other Shareholders decline or fail to respond within the offer period, the Selling Shareholder may proceed with the third-party sale on the same terms, provided the transferee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.
13.3 Permitted Transfers
Notwithstanding the ROFR provisions, Shares may be transferred without triggering ROFR in the following circumstances:
Transfer to immediate family members (spouse, children, parents) or to a trust for the benefit of the Shareholder or their family, provided the transferee agrees to be bound by this Agreement
Transfer pursuant to operation of law (e.g., inheritance, bankruptcy), provided legal heirs or successors agree to be bound by this Agreement
13.4 Binding Effect on Transferees
Any permitted transferee of Shares must execute a deed of adherence agreeing to be bound by all terms and conditions of this Agreement as a Shareholder.
ARTICLE 14: TAG-ALONG AND DRAG-ALONG RIGHTS
14.1 Tag-Along Rights
If a Shareholder holding more than fifty percent (50%) of the Shares (“Selling Shareholder”) proposes to sell their Shares to a third party, the following tag-along rights shall apply. For the avoidance of doubt, any sale that constitutes a sale of the Company or all or substantially all of its assets is also a Category B Reserved Matter under Article 6.2 requiring unanimous consent, and these tag-along provisions operate in conjunction with that requirement:
The Majority Shareholder shall notify all other Shareholders ("Minority Shareholders") in writing of the proposed sale, including price and terms
Each Minority Shareholder shall have the right to participate in the sale and sell their Shares to the same purchaser on the same terms and conditions, pro-rata to their shareholding
Minority Shareholders must exercise this right within fifteen (15) days of receiving notice
14.2 Drag-Along Rights
If Shareholders holding more than seventy-five percent (75%) of the Shares agree to sell all or substantially all of the Company's shares or assets to a third party (note: given the current 60-40 shareholding, a 75% threshold effectively requires both Shareholders to agree, and any such transaction independently requires unanimous consent as a Category B Reserved Matter under Article 6.2):
Such Shareholders may require all other Shareholders to sell their Shares on the same terms and conditions
The dragging Shareholders shall provide at least thirty (30) days' written notice to dragged Shareholders
All Shareholders shall receive the same per-share consideration and be subject to the same terms, conditions, and representations
Dragged Shareholders shall cooperate in good faith and execute all documents reasonably necessary to consummate the transaction

ARTICLE 18: DEADLOCK RESOLUTION
18.1 Definition of Deadlock
A deadlock shall be deemed to exist if the Shareholders or Board cannot reach agreement on a Reserved Matter (as defined in Article 6.2), after the consultation and objection process in Article 6.3 has been completed where applicable, and good faith negotiations have continued for a further period of thirty (30) days without resolution.
18.2 Deadlock Resolution Process
When a deadlock arises, the following escalation process shall apply. The resolution path differs depending on whether the matter is a Category A or Category B Reserved Matter, reflecting the balance between majority authority and minority protection established in Article 6.2:
Step 1 — Negotiation (15 days): The Shareholders shall meet in good faith for up to fifteen (15) days in an attempt to resolve the deadlock.
Step 2 — Mediation (30 days): If Step 1 fails, the Shareholders shall agree upon a mutually acceptable mediator within fifteen (15) days and undertake mediation for a period not exceeding thirty (30) days. Mediation shall be conducted in Gurgaon, Haryana, India, in English. The mediation process and all communications within it shall be confidential and without prejudice.
Step 3 — Resolution by matter category: If mediation fails, the outcome is governed as follows. (a) Category A deadlock: The Shareholder holding more than fifty percent (50%) of the issued share capital shall have the right to determine the outcome and proceed with the Category A matter. This right is subject to: (i) compliance with the good faith duty in Article 6.5; (ii) solvency and statutory compliance; and (iii) the mandatory documentation requirement in Article 6.3. This reflects the Majority Shareholder’s legitimate operational authority. (b) Category B deadlock: The majority-decide rule does not apply. Neither Shareholder may unilaterally proceed with or implement a Category B matter during or after a deadlock. The Parties shall escalate to the Buy-Sell mechanism in Step 4.
Step 4 — Buy-Sell Option (Category B deadlocks only): As a last resort for unresolved Category B deadlocks, either Shareholder may initiate a buy-sell mechanism as follows: (a) the initiating Shareholder delivers a written offer specifying a price per share and offering to either buy all Shares held by the other Shareholder, or sell all of their own Shares to the other Shareholder, at that price; (b) the receiving Shareholder must, within thirty (30) days of receipt, elect in writing to either buy or sell at the stated price; (c) the transaction shall close within sixty (60) days of the election, subject to agreed payment terms and compliance with applicable law; (d) if the receiving Shareholder fails to elect within the thirty (30) day period, the initiating Shareholder’s offer to sell shall be deemed accepted and the transaction shall proceed on that basis.
18.3 Interim Measures During Deadlock
While any deadlock resolution process is underway under this Article, neither Shareholder shall take any action that would materially alter the status quo of the Company, including incurring significant new financial obligations, disposing of material assets, changing key personnel, or implementing the disputed action, without the prior written consent of the other Shareholder. This restriction shall not apply where inaction would cause imminent and demonstrable harm to the Company or where required by statutory obligation.
