## 1. Sales Philosophy

Layaa AI sells transformation, not technology. Every engagement must demonstrably improve the client's decision-making capability and profit trajectory. We are not a vendor; we are an enablement partner for Indian SMEs navigating AI adoption.

**Core Principles:**

- Lead with value, not features. Quantify the business outcome before discussing the solution.
- Respect the SME founder's time -- they are operators, not evaluators. Be concise.
- Never over-promise. Under-commit and over-deliver on timelines and outcomes.
- Price reflects value created, not hours consumed.
- If a deal does not make financial sense for both parties, walk away cleanly.

## 3. Lead Qualification Framework

### BANT Criteria

Every lead must be scored against all four BANT dimensions before progressing past MQL.

#### Budget

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 3 -- Strong | Confirmed budget ≥ ₹1L; can pay 50% advance on signing |
| 2 -- Moderate | Budget exists but unallocated; needs internal approval ≤ 2 weeks |
| 1 -- Weak | No dedicated budget; "exploring"; needs board/investor approval |
| 0 -- Disqualify | Explicitly stated budget < ₹50K (below minimum viable deal) |

#### Authority

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 3 -- Strong | Direct contact is the decision maker (Founder, MD, CTO) |
| 2 -- Moderate | Contact is a strong influencer with direct access to decision maker |
| 1 -- Weak | Contact is a researcher/junior employee with no buying power |
| 0 -- Disqualify | Cannot identify or access the decision maker after 2 attempts |

#### Need

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 3 -- Strong | Articulated a specific, measurable problem AI can solve; quantified impact |
| 2 -- Moderate | Recognises a general inefficiency; open to AI but hasn't scoped the problem |
| 1 -- Weak | "Curious about AI"; no defined problem; FOMO-driven inquiry |
| 0 -- Disqualify | No identifiable need; looking for free consultation |

#### Timeline

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 3 -- Strong | Wants to start within 30 days; has internal deadline driving urgency |
| 2 -- Moderate | Planning for next quarter; 30-90 day horizon |
| 1 -- Weak | "Sometime this year"; no internal deadline |
| 0 -- Disqualify | "Just exploring for future"; no timeline commitment after probing |

**Qualification Threshold:**
- Total ≥ 9/12 -- Proceed to SQL immediately
- Total 6-8 -- Nurture with content; re-qualify in 2 weeks
- Total ≤ 5 -- Park in marketing nurture; do not invest sales time

## 4. Sales Process -- Stage by Stage

### Stage Overview

```
Lead → MQL → SQL → Discovery → Proposal → Negotiation → Won/Lost
```

### Stage 1: Lead Generation

**Owner:** Marketing + Inbound channels
**Activities:**
- Website enquiries, LinkedIn content engagement, referrals, event leads
- Initial form fill or conversation captured in CRM

**Exit Criteria:** Contact information captured; basic firmographic data available.

### Stage 2: MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)

**Owner:** Marketing
**Activities:**
- Lead scoring applied (firmographic + behavioural)
- ICP match confirmed (industry, size, geography)
- Initial outreach email/message sent

**Exit Criteria:** ICP score ≥ 60; engagement signal received (email reply, call booked, content download).

**Conversion Benchmark:** MQL → SQL = 25%

### Stage 3: SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)

**Owner:** Sales (AE)
**Activities:**
- BANT qualification call (15-20 minutes)
- Confirm budget range, authority, specific need, timeline
- Score ≥ 9/12 on BANT

**Exit Criteria:** BANT-qualified; decision maker identified; discovery call scheduled.

**Conversion Benchmark:** SQL → Proposal = 60%

### Stage 4: Discovery

**Owner:** Sales (AE) + Solutions Architect (if technical)
**Activities:**
- Full discovery call using Arjun's 5-Phase Methodology (see Section 5)
- Current state assessment
- Pain quantification
- Solution mapping

**Exit Criteria:** Discovery summary documented; solution approach agreed; client confirms interest in proposal.

### Stage 5: Proposal

**Owner:** Sales (AE)
**Activities:**
- Proposal drafted per Layaa AI template
- Internal review (deals > ₹3L require Founder review)
- Proposal presented (always live, never just emailed)
- Q&A session

**Exit Criteria:** Proposal delivered and discussed; client feedback received; next steps agreed.

**Conversion Benchmark:** Proposal → Won = 35%

### Stage 6: Negotiation

**Owner:** Sales (AE) + Founder (for deals > ₹5L or discount > 10%)
**Activities:**
- Scope adjustments
- Pricing discussion
- Contract terms negotiation
- Payment terms agreement

**Exit Criteria:** Commercial terms agreed; SoW drafted; legal review complete (if applicable).

### Stage 7: Won / Lost

**Won:**
- SoW signed
- 50% advance payment received
- Handoff to delivery initiated (see Section 11)

**Lost:**
- Loss reason documented (mandatory)
- Post-mortem within 48 hours
- Lead moved to re-engagement nurture (90-day follow-up)

**End-to-End Conversion:** ~5.25% (Lead to Won)

## 5. Discovery Call Framework -- Arjun's 5-Phase Methodology

The discovery call is the most critical touchpoint. It determines deal quality, solution fit, and client trust. Follow this framework rigorously.

**Total Duration:** 45-60 minutes

### Phase 1: Context Setting (5 minutes)

**Objective:** Establish rapport and set expectations.

**Script Framework:**
- Thank them for their time. Acknowledge their business briefly (show you did homework).
- State the purpose: "I'd like to understand your current operations and challenges so we can explore whether AI automation is a good fit -- and if so, what that might look like."
- Set the agenda: "I'll ask questions for about 30 minutes, then share relevant examples, and we'll discuss next steps."
- Get permission: "Does that work for you? Anything you'd like to add to the agenda?"

**Key Behaviours:**
- Mirror their energy. If they are formal, be formal. If casual, match it.
- Name-drop only if you have a genuine reference in their industry.
- Never start with a pitch.

### Phase 2: Current State Mapping (15 minutes)

**Objective:** Understand their business operations, workflows, and existing technology.

**Question Bank:**

*Business Context:*
- "Walk me through your core business model -- how do you generate revenue?"
- "What does a typical week look like for your team? Where do they spend most of their time?"
- "What tools and systems do you currently use for [operations/finance/customer management]?"

*Process Deep Dive:*
- "Can you describe the workflow for [specific process relevant to their ICP]?"
- "How many people are involved in [process]? What are the manual steps?"
- "What happens when [process] breaks down or gets delayed?"

*Data Readiness:*
- "Where does your data currently live -- spreadsheets, software, paper?"
- "How do you currently make decisions about [pricing/inventory/hiring/clients]?"
- "If you could get any report or insight instantly, what would it be?"

**What to Listen For:**
- Repeated frustrations (these are the real pain points)
- Workarounds they have built (signals unmet needs)
- Data sources they mention (determines technical feasibility)
- Emotional language ("nightmare", "waste of time", "we're drowning") -- mark these for the proposal

### Phase 3: Pain Quantification (10 minutes)

**Objective:** Attach numbers to the problems identified. This is what justifies the price.

**Question Bank:**
- "You mentioned [process] takes [X hours/week]. How many people are involved?"
- "What does that translate to in cost? Roughly ₹[estimate] per month?"
- "When [problem] happens, what's the downstream impact? Lost revenue? Delayed delivery? Client churn?"
- "If we could reduce [process time] by 60%, what would that mean for your business in the next 12 months?"
- "How much are you spending today on tools/people to manage [process]?"

**Pain Quantification Formula:**

```
Annual Cost of Problem = (Hours/week x People involved x Hourly cost x 52) + (Revenue lost per incident x Incidents/year) + (Opportunity cost of management attention)
```

**Target:** Establish that the problem costs at least 3-5x the proposed solution price annually.

### Phase 4: Solution Alignment (10 minutes)

**Objective:** Map Layaa AI capabilities to their specific pains. Not a product demo -- a solution conversation.

**Approach:**
- "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm thinking..."
- Map each pain point to a specific Layaa AI service/product:
  - Workflow automation pain → Layaa OS / Pre-built automations
  - Knowledge/training pain → EduFlow
  - Compliance/accounting pain → CA AI Agent
  - Data/decision-making pain → Custom AI development
- Share one relevant case study or example (keep to 2 minutes max)
- Validate: "Does this resonate with what you're looking for?"

**Rules:**
- Never discuss pricing in the discovery call unless directly asked.
- If asked about price, say: "I want to give you an accurate number, not a range -- let me scope this properly and come back with a proposal within [3-5] business days."
- Maximum 2 products/services discussed -- do not overwhelm.

### Phase 5: Next Steps and Close (5 minutes)

**Objective:** Secure commitment to the next stage.

**Script Framework:**
- Summarise the 3 key pain points and the proposed approach in 30 seconds.
- Propose next step: "I'd like to put together a detailed proposal covering [scope]. Can we schedule a 30-minute call next [day] to walk through it?"
- Confirm stakeholders: "Who else should be part of the proposal discussion?"
- Set timeline: "I'll have the proposal to you by [date]. Does that timeline work?"
- Close with: "Anything else I should know before I start working on this?"

**Post-Call Actions (within 2 hours):**
1. Send a thank-you message summarising key discussion points.
2. Log detailed notes in CRM -- pain points, quoted costs, stakeholders, next steps.
3. Flag technical requirements for Solutions Architect if needed.
4. Begin proposal draft.

## 6. Proposal Creation

### Proposal Structure

Every Layaa AI proposal follows this template:

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Client's situation and key challenges (in their words)
   - Proposed approach (2-3 sentences)
   - Expected outcomes with quantified impact
   - Investment summary

2. **Understanding Your Challenges** (1-2 pages)
   - Detailed problem statement using discovery insights
   - Cost of inaction (quantified from Phase 3)
   - Industry context and benchmarks

3. **Proposed Solution** (2-3 pages)
   - Solution overview mapped to each challenge
   - Layaa AI methodology: Discovery → Assessment → Development → Validation → Enablement
   - Deliverables list (be specific)
   - Technology stack overview (Layaa OS, EduFlow, CA AI Agent as applicable)
   - Timeline with milestones

4. **Investment** (1 page)
   - Implementation fee (broken by phase if > ₹2L)
   - Monthly retainer recommendation
   - Payment terms: 50% advance, balance on milestones
   - GST note: All prices + 18% GST

5. **Why Layaa AI** (1 page)
   - Team credentials
   - Relevant experience
   - Client testimonials/case studies
   - SME-specific approach differentiator

6. **Next Steps** (half page)
   - Acceptance process
   - Onboarding timeline
   - Key contacts

### Proposal Rules

- Proposals must be presented live (video call or in-person). Never just email.
- Maximum 48-hour turnaround after discovery call for standard deals.
- 72-hour turnaround for complex/enterprise deals.
- All proposals > ₹3L require internal review before sending.
- Include a validity period (14 days standard, 30 days for enterprise).

## 7. Negotiation Tactics

### Principles

1. **Never negotiate against yourself.** Wait for the client to make the first counter.
2. **Trade, don't concede.** Every discount must come with a scope reduction or term extension.
3. **Anchor high.** Start with the full-value proposal. Leave room for negotiation.
4. **Protect margins.** Minimum 30% margin on any deal. Non-negotiable.

### Approved Negotiation Levers

| Lever | Conditions | Approval |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| 5-10% discount | Annual prepayment or 2+ module commitment | AE authority |
| 11-15% discount | Annual prepayment AND case study rights AND referral commitment | Sales Lead authority |
| 16-20% discount | Strategic account with documented LTV > ₹10L | Founder approval required |
| >20% discount | NEVER without Founder explicit approval; must justify in writing | Founder approval mandatory |
| Extended payment terms (45 days) | Clean credit history; deal > ₹3L | Sales Lead authority |
| Extended payment terms (60 days) | Enterprise only; deal > ₹5L | Founder approval required |
| >60 day payment terms | NOT PERMITTED -- red flag, walk away | N/A |
| Phased implementation | Any deal; de-risks both sides | AE authority |
| Pilot/proof-of-concept | ₹50K minimum; 4-week max; clear success criteria | Sales Lead authority |

### Common Negotiation Scenarios

**"Your price is too high"**
- Response: "I understand. Let's look at the value side -- based on our discussion, [problem] is costing you approximately ₹[X] annually. Our solution at ₹[Y] pays for itself in [Z] months. Would it help to phase the implementation to spread the investment?"

**"Competitor X is cheaper"**
- Response: "I respect that. Can you share what's included in their proposal? Often the difference is in post-deployment support, SME-specific customisation, and the enablement methodology. We find that cheaper implementations frequently cost more in rework. That said, if there's a specific scope adjustment that brings us in line, I'm happy to explore it."

**"Can you do a free pilot?"**
- Response: "We don't offer free pilots because we've found they don't get the internal attention needed to evaluate properly. We do offer a paid proof-of-concept starting at ₹50K with clear success criteria. If the POC doesn't meet the agreed metrics, we'll credit the amount toward the full engagement."

**"We need to think about it"**
- Response: "Of course. To help with your evaluation, what specific concerns should I address? And who else in your team would benefit from a brief walkthrough? I'd suggest we reconnect on [specific date] -- does [day] at [time] work?"

## 8. Closing Techniques

### The Assumptive Close
After all objections are addressed, move directly to logistics.
- "Great. I'll send over the SoW today. Shall I address it to you or your finance team for the advance payment?"

### The Summary Close
Restate all agreed points and ask for commitment.
- "So we've agreed on [scope], starting [date], with the [Growth] retainer. The investment is ₹[X] implementation plus ₹[Y]/month. I'll prepare the SoW -- can we target signing by [date]?"

### The Urgency Close (use sparingly and honestly)
- "Our delivery team has capacity for a [month] start. If we finalise by [date], I can hold that slot. After that, the next available window is [later date]."

### The Puppy Dog Close (for hesitant prospects)
- "Let's start with the [smallest module]. You'll see results in [X] weeks, and we can expand from there. No long-term lock-in on the retainer -- monthly terms."

### The Reference Close
- "Would it help to speak with [reference client in same industry]? They had similar concerns and can share their experience directly."

### Close Timing Signals
Watch for these buying signals to initiate the close:
- Client asks about implementation timeline
- Client asks about team requirements on their side
- Client forwards the proposal to others internally
- Client asks for contract/SoW details
- Client negotiates on specific terms (negotiation = interest)

## 9. Objection Handling by ICP

### SaaS Startups

| Objection | Response Strategy |
|-----------|------------------|
| "We can build this in-house" | "Absolutely, and with your engineering team, you could. The question is: should your developers spend 3-4 months on internal tooling, or ship product features that drive revenue? We deliver in 4-6 weeks, and your team stays focused on your core product." |
| "We use [Zapier/Make] already" | "Those are great for simple workflows. Where we add value is in the AI layer -- not just moving data between tools, but making intelligent decisions within those workflows. Think of us as the brain on top of your existing automation stack." |
| "We're pre-revenue / tight on cash" | "I get it. Let's look at a phased approach -- start with one high-impact automation that demonstrably saves time or reduces churn. The ₹50K starting point gives you a tangible win to justify further investment." |
| "How do you handle our data security?" | "We follow enterprise-grade data handling practices. All data stays within your infrastructure or India-hosted cloud. We can sign an NDA and data processing agreement before any technical work begins." |

### Logistics SMEs

| Objection | Response Strategy |
|-----------|------------------|
| "Our team won't adopt new technology" | "That's exactly why our methodology includes an Enablement phase. We don't just deploy and leave -- we train your team on the ground, create SOPs in Hindi/English, and provide 30 days of hand-holding support." |
| "We've tried software before and it failed" | "I hear that often. Usually the failure was in implementation, not the technology. Our approach starts with understanding your exact workflow -- not forcing you into a template. Can you share what went wrong last time?" |
| "Excel works fine for us" | "Excel is powerful, and we don't replace it -- we make it smarter. Imagine your dispatch planning sheet updating itself based on real-time demand predictions. Same interface your team knows, but with AI working behind the scenes." |
| "This seems expensive for our business" | "Let's look at it differently. You mentioned [X] hours/week on [process]. At your team's cost, that's approximately ₹[Y]/month. Our solution at ₹[Z]/month replaces that and scales without adding headcount." |

### Fintech Companies

| Objection | Response Strategy |
|-----------|------------------|
| "We need RBI/regulatory compliance" | "Our CA AI Agent is designed with Indian regulatory frameworks in mind. We work with compliance consultants to ensure every automation meets current guidelines. We can include a compliance review milestone in the project plan." |

### Professional Services

| Objection | Response Strategy |
|-----------|------------------|

| "Our clients expect a personal touch" | "And they should continue to get one. AI handles the back-office and preparation work. Your client-facing interactions actually improve because your team is better prepared and less rushed." |
| "We're a small firm -- this seems like enterprise tech" | "That's the gap we fill. Enterprise AI is expensive and complex. Layaa AI packages are designed for firms your size -- starting at ₹50K implementation with ₹15K/month retainers. You get enterprise capability at SME pricing." |
| "How long before we see ROI?" | "For professional services firms, we typically see measurable time savings within 4-6 weeks of deployment. Full ROI -- where the monthly savings exceed the monthly cost -- usually lands in month 2-3 of the retainer." |

## 11. Handoff to Delivery

### Trigger

Handoff is initiated when ALL of the following are true:
- SoW signed by client
- 50% advance payment received and confirmed
- Client brief document completed
- Delivery team capacity confirmed

### Handoff Package (mandatory)

The AE must prepare and submit the following to the delivery team:

1. **Signed Statement of Work** -- fully executed copy
2. **Payment Confirmation** -- advance receipt from finance
3. **Client Brief** -- using the standard template:
   - Company background and context
   - Key stakeholders and contact details
   - Problem statement (from discovery)
   - Agreed scope and deliverables
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Client's technical environment
   - Success criteria (quantified)
   - Known risks or constraints
4. **Discovery Call Notes** -- raw notes from the 5-Phase call
5. **Proposal Document** -- final version as shared with client
6. **Communication Preferences** -- client's preferred channel, meeting cadence, escalation contacts

### Handoff Meeting

A 30-minute internal handoff meeting is mandatory:
- **Attendees:** AE, Delivery Lead, Solutions Architect (if applicable), CTO (for deals > ₹5L)
- **Agenda:** Walk through the client brief, flag risks, confirm delivery timeline, assign project owner
- **Output:** Delivery kickoff date confirmed; client introduction email sent same day

### Post-Handoff AE Responsibilities

- Join the client kickoff call (first 15 minutes)
- Monthly check-in with client for the first 3 months
- Responsible for upsell identification throughout engagement
- Remain the relationship owner for contract renewals

## 12. Red Flags -- When to Walk Away

### Financial Red Flags

| Red Flag | Threshold | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Gross margin below target | < 30% | Do not proceed. Restructure scope or walk away. |
| CAC to LTV ratio | < 2.5x | Re-evaluate deal economics. If LTV cannot be improved, decline. |
| Payment terms requested | > 60 days | Decline. Maximum 60 days for enterprise with Founder approval. |
| Discount demand | > 20% | Requires Founder written approval. If no strategic justification, walk away. |
| Client wants free work | Any | "We're happy to do a paid POC. We don't do free work." |
| Advance payment resistance | Won't pay 50% advance | Non-negotiable. Offer escrow if they have trust concerns. |

### Operational Red Flags

| Red Flag | Signal | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Client concentration risk | Would exceed 30% of revenue | Decline or defer until portfolio diversifies. |
| Scope creep in pre-sales | Adding requirements after every call | Reset scope formally. If persistent, walk away. |
| Unrealistic timeline | Wants 4-week delivery for 12-week scope | Educate. If they insist, decline. |
| No clear decision maker | 3+ calls without authority figure | Insist on decision-maker meeting or disengage. |
| Technology mismatch | Requires capabilities outside Layaa AI's stack | Refer to partner. Don't take on work you can't deliver. |

### Behavioural Red Flags

| Red Flag | Signal | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Disrespectful to team | Rude behaviour in calls, emails | One warning. Second instance = disengage. |
| NDA/contract avoidance | Refuses standard legal terms | Potential fraud risk. Escalate to Founder. |
| History of vendor-hopping | 3+ vendors in 2 years for same problem | Deep-dive on why. Likely a scope/expectation issue on their side. |
| Competitor information fishing | Asks about Layaa AI's clients, pricing strategy, technology in unusual detail | Limit information. Steer to their needs or disengage. |
