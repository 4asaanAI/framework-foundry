## 1. Purpose

This document provides a library of reusable legal clauses for Layaa AI Private Limited contracts. Each clause is drafted for direct insertion into agreements with appropriate customisation for deal-specific terms. Clauses are written to comply with Indian law, including the Indian Contract Act 1872, IT Act 2000, DPDP Act 2023, and Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996.

**Usage Notes:**
- Placeholder text appears in `[SQUARE BRACKETS]` and must be replaced with deal-specific values.
- Where multiple options are offered, select the appropriate variant.
- These clauses are starting points. Engage legal counsel for high-value or complex engagements.

## 2. Limitation of Liability

### 2.1 Standard Limitation (Capped at Contract Value)

```
LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

(a) Subject to Clause [X.X(c)] below, the total aggregate liability of either Party
    to the other Party under or in connection with this Agreement, whether in
    contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise,
    shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable under this Agreement (or the
    relevant Statement of Work) during the twelve (12) month period immediately
    preceding the event giving rise to such liability (the "Liability Cap").

(b) Neither Party shall be liable to the other Party for any:
    (i)   loss of profits, revenue, or anticipated savings;
    (ii)  loss of business or business opportunity;
    (iii) loss of or damage to goodwill or reputation;
    (iv)  loss of data (except to the extent covered by data protection obligations);
    (v)   indirect, incidental, consequential, special, punitive, or exemplary damages,
    in each case, whether or not such losses were foreseeable or the Party had been
    advised of the possibility of such losses.

(c) Nothing in this Agreement shall limit or exclude either Party's liability for:
    (i)   fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
    (ii)  death or personal injury caused by negligence;
    (iii) wilful misconduct or gross negligence;
    (iv)  breach of confidentiality obligations under Clause [X];
    (v)   breach of data protection obligations under Clause [X];
    (vi)  infringement of the other Party's Intellectual Property rights;
    (vii) any liability which cannot be limited or excluded under applicable law.
```

### 2.2 Variant -- Per-SoW Cap (for MSA Engagements)

```
Where this Agreement governs multiple Statements of Work, the Liability Cap shall
apply separately to each Statement of Work based on the fees paid or payable under
that specific Statement of Work during the relevant twelve (12) month period.
```

## 3. Indemnification

### 3.1 Mutual Indemnification

```
INDEMNIFICATION

(a) Each Party ("Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the
    other Party, its directors, officers, and employees ("Indemnified Party") from
    and against any and all third-party claims, demands, actions, losses, damages,
    liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising out of
    or in connection with:
    (i)   any breach of this Agreement by the Indemnifying Party;
    (ii)  any negligent or wrongful act or omission of the Indemnifying Party;
    (iii) any infringement or alleged infringement of third-party Intellectual
          Property rights by materials provided by the Indemnifying Party.

(b) The Indemnified Party shall:
    (i)   promptly notify the Indemnifying Party in writing of any claim;
    (ii)  give the Indemnifying Party sole control of the defence and settlement
          of such claim (provided that the Indemnifying Party shall not settle any
          claim that imposes obligations on the Indemnified Party without prior
          written consent);
    (iii) provide reasonable cooperation at the Indemnifying Party's expense.

(c) The indemnification obligations under this Clause are subject to the Liability
    Cap set out in Clause [X], except in cases of fraud, wilful misconduct, or
    breach of confidentiality.
```

### 3.2 Layaa AI IP Indemnity (for Client-Facing Agreements)

```
Layaa AI shall indemnify and hold harmless the Client against any third-party claim
that the Deliverables, as delivered by Layaa AI and used by the Client in accordance

(a) the claim does not arise from (i) Client-provided materials, (ii) modifications

(b) the Client provides prompt notice and reasonable cooperation; and
(c) Layaa AI has sole control of the defence and settlement.

If a Deliverable is found to infringe, Layaa AI shall, at its option and expense:
(i)   obtain the right for the Client to continue using the Deliverable;
(ii)  modify the Deliverable to make it non-infringing; or
(iii) replace the Deliverable with a non-infringing equivalent.

If none of the above remedies is commercially practicable, either Party may terminate
the relevant Statement of Work and Layaa AI shall refund fees paid for the infringing
Deliverable.
```

## 4. Confidentiality

### 4.1 Comprehensive Confidentiality Clause

```
CONFIDENTIALITY

1. Definition. "Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by one Party
   ("Discloser") to the other Party ("Recipient"), whether in writing, orally, visually,
   or electronically, that (a) is marked or designated as confidential or proprietary, or
   (b) a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the
   information and circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without
   limitation: business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, customer and
   supplier lists, technical data, source code, algorithms, product roadmaps, trade
   secrets, and personnel information.

2. Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that:
   (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Recipient;
   (b) was already known to the Recipient prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written
       records;
   (c) is independently developed by the Recipient without reference to the Discloser's
       Confidential Information;
   (d) is lawfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

3. Obligations. The Recipient shall:
   (a) use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its
       obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;
   (b) protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it
       uses for its own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable
       care;
   (c) restrict access to Confidential Information to its employees, directors,
       contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and are bound by
       confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those herein;
   (d) not disclose, copy, or distribute Confidential Information to any third party
       without the Discloser's prior written consent.

4. Compelled Disclosure. If the Recipient is compelled by law, regulation, or legal
   process to disclose Confidential Information, the Recipient shall (to the extent
   legally permitted) promptly notify the Discloser and cooperate to minimise the
   scope of disclosure. Such compelled disclosure shall not constitute a breach.

5. Return/Destruction. Upon termination of this Agreement or upon the Discloser's
   written request, the Recipient shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential
   Information and certify such destruction in writing. The Recipient may retain
   copies required by law or its standard backup procedures, subject to continued
   confidentiality obligations.

6. Duration. The obligations under this Clause shall survive termination of this
   Agreement for a period of three (3) years, except for trade secrets, which shall
   be protected for as long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

7. Remedies. The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this Clause may cause
   irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. The Discloser
   shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the
   necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond.
```

```

```
INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY -- HYBRID

(a) "Foreground IP" means Intellectual Property created specifically for the Client
    under this Agreement that has no utility outside the Client's specific use case.
    Foreground IP is assigned to the Client upon full payment.

(b) "Background IP" means all Intellectual Property owned by or licensed to Layaa AI
    prior to this Agreement, as well as general-purpose tools, frameworks,
    libraries, methodologies, and know-how used or developed during this engagement

(c) To the extent Background IP is incorporated into the Deliverables, the Client is
    granted a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free, non-transferable licence to use

    Deliverables.

(d) Layaa AI retains the right to use general knowledge, skills, experience, ideas,
    concepts, techniques, and know-how acquired or developed during this engagement,
    provided that such use does not disclose the Client's Confidential Information.
```

## 6. Non-Solicitation

### 6.1 Standard Non-Solicitation (Compliant with Section 27, Indian Contract Act)

```
NON-SOLICITATION

(a) During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months
    following its termination or expiry ("Restricted Period"), neither Party shall,
    directly or indirectly, solicit, induce, or attempt to solicit or induce any
    employee, contractor, or consultant of the other Party to terminate their
    engagement with that Party.

(b) This restriction does not apply to:
    (i)   general recruitment advertisements or postings not specifically targeted
          at the other Party's personnel;
    (ii)  unsolicited applications from the other Party's personnel;
    (iii) persons whose engagement with the other Party has already ended at the
          time of approach.

(c) The Parties acknowledge that this Clause constitutes a reasonable restriction
    on solicitation (and not a restraint of trade) in accordance with Section 27
    of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.
```

> **Legal Note:** Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act 1872 renders agreements in restraint of trade void (with limited exceptions for sale of goodwill). Broad non-compete clauses are generally unenforceable in India. Layaa AI uses non-solicitation clauses (which are narrower and more likely enforceable) rather than non-compete clauses.

## 7. Force Majeure

```
FORCE MAJEURE

(a) Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its
    obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such failure or delay is
    caused by a Force Majeure Event.

(b) "Force Majeure Event" means any event beyond the reasonable control of the
    affected Party, including but not limited to: natural disasters (earthquake,
    flood, epidemic, pandemic), acts of war or terrorism, government actions or
    sanctions, labour disputes (not involving the affected Party's employees),
    fire, power failure, internet or telecommunications failure, and cyberattacks
    of a nature and scale beyond reasonable preventive measures.

(c) The affected Party shall:
    (i)   notify the other Party in writing within five (5) business days of
          becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event;
    (ii)  provide reasonable details of the event and its expected duration;
    (iii) use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and resume performance.

(d) If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days,
    either Party may terminate this Agreement (or the affected Statement of Work)
    by giving written notice. Upon such termination, the Client shall pay for
    Services satisfactorily performed up to the date of the Force Majeure Event.

(e) For the avoidance of doubt, the following shall not constitute Force Majeure
    Events: changes in market conditions, financial hardship, inability to obtain
    financing, loss of key personnel, or failure of a Party's subcontractors
    (unless such failure is itself caused by a Force Majeure Event).
```

## 9. Data Protection (DPDP Act 2023 Compliant)

```
DATA PROTECTION

1. Compliance. Both Parties shall comply with applicable data protection laws,
   including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act"), the
   Information Technology Act, 2000, and associated rules and regulations.

2. Processing. Where Layaa AI processes Personal Data on behalf of the Client:
   (a) the Client is the Data Fiduciary and Layaa AI is the Data Processor;
   (b) Layaa AI shall process Personal Data only in accordance with the Client's
       documented instructions and the terms of the Data Processing Agreement
       (if executed) or this Clause;
   (c) Layaa AI shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures
       to protect Personal Data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration,
       or destruction.

3. Consent. The Client warrants that it has obtained all necessary consents from
   Data Principals for the processing of their Personal Data as contemplated under
   this Agreement, in compliance with Section 6 of the DPDP Act.

4. Purpose Limitation. Layaa AI shall process Personal Data only for the specific
   purposes set out in this Agreement or the relevant Statement of Work. Personal
   Data shall not be used for any other purpose, including training AI models,
   without the Client's express written consent.

5. Data Minimisation. Layaa AI shall process only the minimum Personal Data
   necessary for the stated purpose.

6. Data Breach. In the event of a Personal Data Breach:
   (a) Layaa AI shall notify the Client without undue delay and in any event within
       twenty-four (24) hours of becoming aware of the breach;
   (b) such notification shall include: the nature of the breach, categories of data
       affected, approximate number of Data Principals affected, likely consequences,
       and measures taken or proposed to mitigate;
   (c) the Client shall be responsible for notifying the Data Protection Board of
       India within seventy-two (72) hours as required under the DPDP Act;
   (d) Layaa AI shall cooperate fully with the Client in investigating and
       remediating the breach.

7. Cross-Border Transfers. Layaa AI shall not transfer Personal Data to any
   jurisdiction outside India unless:
   (a) such jurisdiction has been notified by the Central Government as a permitted
       jurisdiction under the DPDP Act; or
   (b) the Client has provided prior written consent and appropriate safeguards
       are in place.

8. Sub-Processing. Layaa AI shall not engage any sub-processor to process Personal
   Data without the Client's prior written consent. Layaa AI shall impose data
   protection obligations on sub-processors no less restrictive than those in this
   Agreement.

9. Data Subject Rights. Layaa AI shall assist the Client in responding to requests
   from Data Principals exercising their rights under the DPDP Act, including
   rights of access, correction, and erasure.

10. Deletion. Upon completion of processing or termination of this Agreement,
    Layaa AI shall delete or return all Personal Data (at the Client's election)
    and certify such deletion in writing, unless retention is required by law.

11. Penalties. The Parties acknowledge that violations of the DPDP Act may result in
    penalties up to INR 250 Crore as determined by the Data Protection Board of India.
    Each Party shall bear its own liability for its violations.
```

## 10. Dispute Resolution (Arbitration)

```
DISPUTE RESOLUTION

1. Escalation. The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute through
   good-faith negotiation between their respective authorised representatives.
   If the dispute is not resolved within fifteen (15) days, it shall be escalated
   to the senior management / Founders of each Party for resolution within a
   further fifteen (15) days.

2. Mediation. If the dispute remains unresolved after escalation, the Parties may
   (but are not obligated to) refer the dispute to mediation under the rules of the
   [Indian Institute of Arbitration and Mediation / or other agreed institution].

3. Arbitration. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection
   with this Agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity,
   or termination, which is not resolved through negotiation or mediation, shall be
   referred to and finally resolved by arbitration in accordance with the Arbitration
   and Conciliation Act, 1996, as amended.

   (a) Seat of Arbitration: Gurgaon, Haryana, India.
   (b) Language of Arbitration: English.
   (c) Number of Arbitrators: One (1), to be mutually appointed by the Parties.
       If the Parties fail to agree on the appointment within thirty (30) days of
       a Party's request for arbitration, the arbitrator shall be appointed in
       accordance with the provisions of the Act.
   (d) The arbitral award shall be final and binding on both Parties.
   (e) The costs of arbitration shall be borne as determined by the arbitrator.

4. Interim Relief. Nothing in this Clause shall prevent either Party from seeking
   interim or injunctive relief from a court of competent jurisdiction where
   necessary to prevent irreparable harm.

5. Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance
   with the laws of India.

6. Jurisdiction. Subject to the arbitration provisions above, the courts in Gurgaon,
   Haryana, India shall have exclusive jurisdiction.
```

## 11. Payment Terms

```
PAYMENT TERMS

1. Invoicing. Layaa AI shall issue invoices in accordance with the payment schedule
   set out in the relevant Statement of Work. All invoices shall comply with
   applicable GST requirements and include Layaa AI's GSTIN, the applicable SAC
   code (998314), and GST at the rate of 18%.

2. Due Date. All invoices are due and payable within [fifteen (15)] days of the
   invoice date ("Net 15"), unless otherwise specified in the Statement of Work.

3. Payment Method. Payments shall be made via [bank transfer / UPI / other agreed
   method] to Layaa AI's designated bank account. Details shall be provided on each
   invoice.

4. Late Payment. If any amount remains unpaid after the due date:
   (a) interest shall accrue on the overdue amount at the rate of 1.5% per month
       (or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower), calculated from
       the due date to the date of actual payment;
   (b) Layaa AI may, after providing ten (10) days' written notice, suspend
       performance of Services until all overdue amounts are paid in full.

5. Disputed Invoices. The Client shall notify Layaa AI in writing of any invoice
   dispute within seven (7) days of receipt, specifying the nature and amount of
   the dispute. Undisputed amounts shall be paid by the due date regardless of any
   dispute. The Parties shall resolve disputed amounts in good faith within fifteen
   (15) days.

6. Taxes. All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. GST at the prevailing rate
   (currently 18%) shall be added to each invoice. TDS, if applicable, shall be
   deducted by the Client at the prescribed rate and the Client shall provide TDS
   certificates (Form 16A) within the statutory timeframe.

7. Currency. All amounts are in Indian Rupees (INR) unless otherwise specified in
   the Statement of Work. For international engagements, the applicable currency
   and exchange rate mechanism shall be specified in the SoW.

8. Advance Payment. For new clients or engagements above INR [1,00,000], Layaa AI
   may require an advance payment of [25--50]% of the total project fee prior to
   commencement of work.

9. Milestone Payments. For fixed-fee engagements, payments shall be structured
   against defined milestones. No milestone payment shall be contingent on the
   completion of subsequent milestones.
