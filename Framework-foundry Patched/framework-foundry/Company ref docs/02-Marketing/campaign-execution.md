**Last Updated:** April 2026
**Owner:** Marketing Team, Layaa AI Private Limited

## 1. Purpose

This document provides the operational framework for planning, executing, measuring, and optimizing marketing campaigns at Layaa AI. Every campaign — from a single LinkedIn push to a multi-week product launch — follows this framework to ensure consistency, accountability, and measurable outcomes.

---

## 2. Campaign Types

### 2.1 Awareness Campaigns

**Objective:** Increase brand visibility and recognition among target ICPs.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | Reach new audiences; establish Layaa AI as a credible voice in Indian SME AI |
| **Typical Duration** | 2-4 weeks |
| **Primary Channels** | LinkedIn organic, paid LinkedIn, blog/SEO, Instagram |
| **Key Metrics** | Impressions, reach, new followers, profile visits, branded search volume |
| **CTA Style** | Soft: "Follow for more," "Read the full story," "See how it works" |
| **Content Focus** | Thought leadership, market insights, educational content |
| **Example** | "AI for Indian SMEs" LinkedIn content series — 10 posts over 3 weeks from both founders |

### 2.2 Lead Generation Campaigns

**Objective:** Capture contact information from qualified prospects.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | Generate MQLs (Marketing Qualified Leads) who enter nurture sequences |
| **Typical Duration** | 3-6 weeks |
| **Primary Channels** | Blog/SEO + lead magnets, workshops, LinkedIn + landing pages, email |
| **Key Metrics** | Leads generated, cost per lead (CPL), lead quality score, download/registration rate |
| **CTA Style** | Medium: "Download the guide," "Register for the workshop," "Get your free assessment" |
| **Content Focus** | Practical guides, assessments, workshops, tools |
| **Example** | "AI Readiness Assessment" campaign — whitepaper download with follow-up email sequence |

### 2.3 Nurture Campaigns

**Objective:** Build trust and move existing leads toward a buying decision.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | Increase engagement with existing leads; move from MQL to SQL (Sales Qualified Lead) |
| **Typical Duration** | Ongoing (triggered sequences) or 4-8 week themed campaigns |
| **Primary Channels** | Email, retargeting (if paid active), LinkedIn engagement |
| **Key Metrics** | Email open rates, click rates, reply rates, SQLs generated, time-to-SQL |
| **CTA Style** | Progressive: starts soft ("Read this case study") and escalates ("Book a diagnostic call") |
| **Content Focus** | Case studies, ROI breakdowns, client testimonials, product walkthroughs |
| **Example** | "From Manual to Automated" email series — 5 emails with escalating case study depth |

### 2.4 Conversion Campaigns

**Objective:** Drive qualified prospects to take a buying action.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | Convert SQLs to diagnostic calls, pilots, or paid engagements |
| **Typical Duration** | 2-4 weeks |
| **Primary Channels** | Email, direct outreach, LinkedIn DM, phone/WhatsApp |
| **Key Metrics** | Diagnostic calls booked, pilot signups, conversion rate (SQL to customer), CAC |
| **CTA Style** | Direct: "Book your diagnostic call," "Start your 30-Day Pilot," "Talk to our team" |
| **Content Focus** | Specific offers, ROI projections, risk-reduction messaging (pilots, no contracts) |
| **Example** | "30-Day AI Pilot" launch campaign — multi-channel push with a specific offer and deadline |

### 2.5 Retention & Expansion Campaigns

**Objective:** Increase client satisfaction, reduce churn, drive upsells and referrals.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | Retain existing clients, expand usage, activate referrals |
| **Typical Duration** | Ongoing (monthly touchpoints) or 2-3 week themed campaigns |
| **Primary Channels** | Email, in-product communication, account manager outreach |
| **Key Metrics** | Retention rate, expansion revenue, referral activations, NPS |
| **CTA Style** | Collaborative: "Add another workflow," "Here's a new feature for your team," "Know someone who'd benefit?" |
| **Content Focus** | Product updates, optimization tips, success milestones, referral program |
| **Example** | "Automation Anniversary" — celebrating 6/12 months of partnership with results recap and referral ask |

## 4. Execution Checklist

This checklist applies to every campaign. Check off each item as it is completed.

### 4.1 Pre-Launch (1-2 weeks before)

- [ ] Campaign brief approved by all required stakeholders
- [ ] All content drafted and reviewed against tone-framework.md
- [ ] Founder-attributed content reviewed against founder-voice.md
- [ ] All visuals/graphics created and approved
- [ ] Landing pages built, tested on mobile and desktop
- [ ] Email sequences configured in email platform
- [ ] UTM parameters defined for all trackable links
- [ ] CRM/lead tracking configured (tags, properties, segments)
- [ ] A/B test variations prepared (subject lines, headlines, CTAs)
- [ ] Escalation triggers reviewed (see tone-framework.md Section 8)
- [ ] Team briefed on campaign (sales team knows what's coming and how to respond to inbound)
- [ ] Paid media configured and budgets set (if applicable)
- [ ] Measurement dashboard created or updated

### 4.2 Launch Day

- [ ] All scheduled content confirmed live and functional
- [ ] Links tested (lead magnets downloadable, landing pages loading, forms working)
- [ ] Email sends confirmed (no errors, personalization rendering correctly)
- [ ] Founder personal posts published (not just scheduled — verify they're live)
- [ ] Team notified: "Campaign is live"
- [ ] First-hour monitoring: check for immediate issues (broken links, wrong dates, formatting errors)

### 4.3 During Campaign

- [ ] Daily: Monitor engagement metrics and respond to comments/replies within 4 hours
- [ ] Daily: Check lead flow (are leads being captured and entering the correct sequences?)
- [ ] Every 3 days: Review A/B test results; pause underperforming variants
- [ ] Weekly: Campaign performance review against targets
- [ ] Weekly: Adjust spend/effort allocation based on channel performance
- [ ] As needed: Respond to optimization triggers (see Section 6)

### 4.4 Post-Campaign (within 1 week of end)

- [ ] Compile final performance data
- [ ] Complete campaign retrospective (see Section 7)
- [ ] Archive all campaign assets (copy, graphics, landing pages)
- [ ] Update CRM with final lead tags and attribution
- [ ] Share results with team (brief summary in team channel)
- [ ] Handoff remaining leads to sales with context
- [ ] Document lessons learned for future campaigns

## 5. A/B Testing Framework

### 5.1 What to Test

| Element | Test Approach | Minimum Sample Size |
|---|---|---|
| **Email subject lines** | Test 2 variations on 20% of list; send winner to remaining 80% | 200+ recipients |
| **Email send times** | Test 2 time slots across consecutive campaigns | 2 campaigns minimum |
| **LinkedIn post hooks** | Test different opening lines on similar topics | Compare across 4+ posts |
| **Landing page headlines** | A/B test with traffic split | 200+ visitors |
| **CTA text** | Test 2 CTA variations on the same page or email | 200+ impressions per variant |
| **Lead magnet titles** | Test different titles for the same content | 100+ visitors per variant |
| **Ad creative** | Test 2-3 image/copy combinations | 500+ impressions per variant |

### 5.2 Testing Rules

1. **Test one variable at a time.** If you change both the subject line and the CTA, you won't know which caused the difference.
2. **Define the success metric before launching the test.** Open rate? Click rate? Conversion rate?
3. **Run tests for a statistically meaningful duration.** Don't call a winner after 2 hours.
4. **Document every test and result.** Maintain a shared A/B testing log.
5. **Apply learnings to future campaigns.** If "question" subject lines consistently outperform "statement" subject lines, update the default approach.

## 6. Performance Metrics & Benchmarks

### 6.1 Metric Definitions

| Metric | Definition | Formula |
|---|---|---|
| **Impressions** | Number of times content was displayed | Platform-reported |
| **Reach** | Number of unique accounts that saw the content | Platform-reported |
| **Engagement Rate** | Percentage of impressions that resulted in an interaction | (Reactions + Comments + Shares + Clicks) / Impressions x 100 |
| **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | Percentage of impressions or recipients who clicked a link | Clicks / (Impressions or Delivered Emails) x 100 |
| **CPL (Cost Per Lead)** | Cost to acquire one lead | Total Campaign Cost / Number of Leads |
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | Cost to acquire one paying customer | Total Sales & Marketing Cost / Number of New Customers |
| **MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)** | Lead that meets defined qualification criteria | CRM-tracked |
| **SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)** | MQL that has been verified by sales as a genuine opportunity | CRM-tracked |
| **Conversion Rate** | Percentage of leads/prospects who take the desired action | Conversions / Total Opportunities x 100 |
| **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** | Revenue generated per rupee of ad spend | Revenue Attributed to Campaign / Ad Spend |

## 7. Reporting Cadence

### 7.1 Real-Time Monitoring (During Active Campaigns)

- **Dashboard access:** Marketing team has live access to campaign metrics
- **Alert triggers:** Automated alerts for anomalies (sudden drop in engagement, spike in unsubscribes, broken links)
- **Slack/team channel updates:** Key milestones shared in real-time ("We hit 50 registrations for the workshop")

### 7.2 Weekly Campaign Report

**When:** Every Monday (covering the previous week)
**Audience:** Marketing team + CEO
**Format:** Shared document or dashboard snapshot

**Contents:**
1. Active campaigns: status and key metrics vs. targets
2. Channel performance: summary of each active channel's weekly metrics
3. Lead flow: new leads, pipeline movement, conversions
4. Issues: anything underperforming and proposed adjustments
5. Next week: planned activities and priorities

### 7.3 Monthly Marketing Report

**When:** First week of each month
**Audience:** Leadership team
**Format:** Structured document with commentary

**Contents:**
1. Executive summary: 3-5 key takeaways
2. Metrics dashboard: all KPIs vs. targets and prior month
3. Campaign performance: each campaign's results and ROI
4. Channel performance: comparative analysis across channels
5. Lead pipeline: volume, quality, conversion rates by stage
6. CAC and CPL trends
7. Learnings and optimizations applied
8. Next month's plan and priorities

### 7.4 Quarterly Campaign Retrospective

**When:** End of each quarter
**Audience:** Full team
**Format:** Presentation + discussion

**Contents:**
1. Quarter goals vs. actual performance
2. Best-performing campaigns and why
3. Underperforming campaigns and why
4. A/B test learnings compiled
5. Channel ROI comparison
6. Budget utilization vs. plan
7. Strategic recommendations for next quarter
8. Updated benchmarks based on actual data

## 8. Optimization Triggers

Optimization triggers are predefined conditions that prompt immediate action. When a trigger is met, the marketing team takes the specified action without waiting for the next reporting cycle.

### 8.1 Underperformance Triggers

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Low email open rate** | Open rate <20% on any send | Review subject line; resend to non-openers with new subject within 48 hours |
| **Low email CTR** | CTR <2% with open rate >30% | Review email body copy and CTA; test new CTA in next send |
| **Low landing page conversion** | Conversion rate <5% with 200+ visitors | Review headline, form length, page load speed; A/B test headline |
| **High unsubscribe rate** | >1% on any single email | Review content relevance, send frequency, segmentation accuracy; pause sequence and investigate |
| **Low workshop attendance** | <30% of registrations attend | Review reminder sequence; adjust event timing; survey no-shows |
| **LinkedIn engagement drop** | Engagement rate <2% for 2 consecutive weeks | Audit content mix; test new formats (carousel, video, poll); review posting times |
| **Lead quality decline** | <50% of MQLs convert to SQL for 2 consecutive weeks | Review lead magnet and qualification criteria; tighten targeting |

### 8.2 Overperformance Triggers

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Viral content** | Any post exceeds 3x average engagement | Boost with paid promotion (if budget available); create follow-up content series on the same topic |
| **High-converting email** | CTR >10% | Analyze what worked; replicate approach in future sends; consider making the content evergreen |
| **Workshop oversubscription** | Registrations exceed capacity by 50%+ | Add a second session; capture waitlist for next event; use demand as social proof |
| **Lead surge** | 2x normal weekly lead volume | Alert sales team immediately; ensure nurture sequences can handle volume; prioritize high-quality leads for fast follow-up |

### 8.3 Market Triggers

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Regulatory change** | New government policy affecting target ICPs | Create rapid-response content (blog + LinkedIn + email) within 72 hours; position Layaa AI's relevant capabilities |
| **Competitor move** | Direct competitor launches in our space | Analyze positioning; amplify differentiation in content; brief sales team on competitive talking points |
| **Industry event** | Major industry conference or event | Pre-event content series; live coverage if attending; post-event analysis content |
| **Client success milestone** | Client achieves a notable result | Fast-track case study creation; seek client approval for public sharing; amplify across channels |

## 9. Campaign Asset Management

### 9.1 Asset Organization

All campaign assets are stored in a shared drive with the following structure:

```
/Campaigns
  /[Year]-[Quarter]
    /[Campaign-Name]
      /Brief
      /Content
        /Copy
        /Graphics
        /Video
      /Landing-Pages
      /Emails
      /Analytics
      /Retrospective
```

### 9.2 Asset Reuse Guidelines

- **Evergreen content** (not tied to dates or specific offers) should be tagged for reuse.
- **High-performing content** should be adapted for other channels (see repurposing matrix in channel-playbooks.md).
- **Outdated content** (references old pricing, past events, or discontinued features) must be archived and removed from active rotation.
- **Templates** (graphics, email layouts, landing page structures) should be maintained as reusable components.

### 9.3 Version Control

- All copy goes through a review process: Draft, Review, Approved, Published.
- Graphics follow: Draft, Review, Approved.
- Once published, content is not edited without creating a new version and noting the change.
