## 1. Overview

Layaa AI Private Limited operates across five distinct service verticals, each designed to address a specific stage of the AI adoption journey for Indian SMEs. Together, these verticals form a complete lifecycle — from awareness and education through to production automation and ongoing support.

All service delivery follows the standardised 5-stage methodology (Section 7) and is powered by the Layaa OS multi-agent AI workforce platform.

## 3. Vertical 1: Education & Workforce Enablement

**Target Margin:** 50-60%

### 3.1 Description

Structured AI literacy and capability-building programmes designed to bridge the knowledge gap between SME teams and practical AI adoption. This vertical creates informed buyers for Layaa AI's consulting and automation services while generating standalone revenue.

### 3.2 Service Offerings

| Offering | Description | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Literacy Workshops** | Foundational sessions covering AI concepts, use cases, and business applications relevant to the client's industry | Half-day / Full-day, on-site or virtual |
| **Corporate AI Training** | Multi-session programmes for teams covering hands-on tool usage, prompt engineering, workflow thinking | Multi-week cohort |
| **Executive AI Briefings** | Condensed strategic sessions for business owners and leadership on AI opportunity assessment | 2-3 hour sessions |
| **Train-the-Trainer** | Enablement of internal champions who can sustain AI adoption post-engagement | Multi-session with certification |
| **Custom Certification Programmes** | Industry-specific AI skill certification for workforce upskilling | Structured cohort with assessment |

### 3.3 Delivery Model

- Standardised curriculum modules customised per client industry and maturity level
- Instructor-led delivery (founder or senior team) with AI-generated supplementary materials
- Pre-built slide decks, exercises, and assessment frameworks reduce per-engagement preparation cost
- Post-training enablement resources delivered via Arush (Documentation & Enablement Specialist)

### 3.4 Margin Drivers

- High reuse of core curriculum materials across clients
- Minimal tooling cost (primarily labour and preparation)
- Cohort-based pricing allows revenue scaling without proportional cost increase
- Follow-on conversion to consulting and automation verticals (upsell path)

### 3.5 Key Agents Involved

Arush (content creation), Tara (brand-aligned materials), Mira (positioning), Arjun (client needs assessment), Kshitiz (industry research)

## 8. Five-Stage Delivery Methodology

All client engagements follow the Layaa AI 5-stage delivery methodology. The stages are sequential, with defined entry and exit criteria ensuring quality and alignment at every step.

```
Discovery → Assessment → Development → Validation → Enablement
```

---

### Stage 1: Discovery

**Objective:** Understand the client's business, pain points, current processes, and desired outcomes.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3-7 business days |
| **Lead Agent** | Arjun (Client Strategy & Discovery Specialist) |
| **Supporting Agents** | Kshitiz (industry research), Rohit (initial feasibility), Yuvaan (scope framing) |
| **Activities** | Client intake questionnaire, stakeholder interviews, current-state process mapping, pain point identification, business goal documentation, initial opportunity sizing |
| **Deliverables** | Discovery Brief (client context, pain points, goals, initial opportunity list) |
| **Exit Criteria** | Client signs off on Discovery Brief; business problem is clearly articulated; initial opportunity list is agreed upon |

**Key principles:**

- Listen more than prescribe — this stage is about understanding, not selling
- Document everything — the Discovery Brief becomes the foundation for all subsequent work
- Identify quick wins — opportunities that can demonstrate value within 30 days
- Assess client readiness — team capability, data availability, tool ecosystem, budget constraints
- Flag risks early — technical, organisational, or budgetary risks must surface in Discovery

---

### Stage 2: Assessment

**Objective:** Analyse identified opportunities in depth, validate feasibility, estimate ROI, and produce an actionable implementation plan.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 5-10 business days |
| **Lead Agent** | Kshitiz (Master Research & Data Analyst) |
| **Supporting Agents** | Rohit (feasibility & risk), Veer (pricing & ROI), Ujjawal (technical feasibility), Arjun (client alignment) |
| **Activities** | Detailed process analysis, automation feasibility scoring, technical architecture evaluation, ROI modelling, risk assessment, solution options comparison, resource and timeline estimation |
| **Deliverables** | Assessment Report (feasibility matrix, ROI projections, recommended solution architecture, implementation plan with milestones, risk register) |
| **Exit Criteria** | Client approves Assessment Report; scope, timeline, and commercial terms agreed; SOW (Statement of Work) signed |

**Key principles:**

- Quantify everything — every recommendation must have a projected business impact
- Be honest about limitations — if something is not feasible or not worthwhile, say so
- Present options — give clients choices with trade-offs clearly explained
- Define success metrics — agree on measurable KPIs before development begins
- Secure commitment — commercial agreement must be in place before proceeding to Development

---

### Stage 3: Development

**Objective:** Design, build, test, and iterate on the approved automation solution.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 2-12 weeks (depending on complexity) |
| **Lead Agent** | Dev (Internal Product Manager) |
| **Supporting Agents** | Ujjawal (architecture & specs), Rohit (QA), Arjun (client checkpoints), Arush (documentation) |
| **Activities** | Detailed technical design, workflow development (n8n/Make/Zapier), AI model configuration and prompt engineering, integration development (APIs, webhooks, WebSocket), internal testing, iterative refinement, client demos at milestones |
| **Deliverables** | Working automation workflows, integration configurations, test results, technical documentation |
| **Exit Criteria** | All workflows pass internal QA; client demo at each milestone approved; system ready for UAT |

**Key principles:**

- Build in sprints — deliver working increments, not big-bang releases
- Client visibility — regular demos and checkpoint calls prevent misalignment
- No-code/low-code first — use n8n and similar tools to maximise client maintainability
- Document as you build — Arush creates documentation in parallel with development, not after
- Test continuously — Rohit validates at each sprint, not just at the end

---

### Stage 4: Validation

**Objective:** Confirm that the built solution meets requirements, performs reliably, and delivers the projected business value.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3-7 business days |
| **Lead Agent** | Rohit (QA & Validation Specialist) |
| **Supporting Agents** | Dev (issue triage), Ujjawal (technical fixes), Arjun (client coordination) |
| **Activities** | User Acceptance Testing (UAT) with client team, edge case testing, load/stress testing (where applicable), error handling verification, integration reliability testing, performance benchmarking against agreed KPIs |
| **Deliverables** | Validation Report (test results, issue log, resolution status, performance against KPIs, sign-off recommendation) |
| **Exit Criteria** | All critical and high-priority issues resolved; client signs UAT acceptance; system approved for production deployment |

**Key principles:**

- Client hands on keyboard — UAT must involve the actual end users, not just stakeholders
- Real data — test with production-representative data, not synthetic samples only
- Document every issue — even minor issues go into the log for transparency
- No silent failures — verify that error handling works and alerts are functional
- Performance baseline — establish the benchmark numbers that Maintenance will monitor against

---

### Stage 5: Enablement

**Objective:** Deploy to production, train client teams, hand over documentation, and transition to ongoing support.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 5-10 business days |
| **Lead Agent** | Arush (Documentation & Enablement Specialist) |
| **Supporting Agents** | Arjun (relationship transition), Dev (deployment), Kaiser (monitoring setup) |
| **Activities** | Production deployment, client team training sessions, user guide and SOP delivery, admin training for client-side maintainers, monitoring and alerting setup, support channel establishment, 30-day hypercare period initiation |
| **Deliverables** | Deployed production system, user guides, SOPs, training recordings, admin documentation, support contact and escalation information, hypercare schedule |
| **Exit Criteria** | System live in production; client team trained and confident; documentation delivered; monitoring active; hypercare period commenced; support retainer activated (if applicable) |

**Key principles:**

- Training is not optional — every deployment includes structured training for end users
- Documentation must be standalone — a new team member should be able to understand the system from docs alone
- Hypercare period — 30 days of elevated support post-launch to catch early issues
- Clean handoff — explicit transition from project delivery to support (if Vertical 4 engaged)
- Success measurement — review actual KPIs against projections at 30 and 60 days post-launch

## 9. Methodology Governance

### 9.1 Stage Gate Reviews

Each stage transition requires formal sign-off:

| Transition | Approver | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery to Assessment | Arjun + Founders | Discovery Brief approved by client |
| Assessment to Development | Founders | SOW signed, commercial terms agreed |
| Development to Validation | Dev + Rohit | Internal QA passed, client milestone demos approved |
| Validation to Enablement | Rohit + Founders | UAT accepted, all critical issues resolved |
| Enablement to Support | Arush + Arjun | Training complete, documentation delivered, monitoring active |

### 9.2 Escalation During Delivery

If any stage stalls or encounters a blocker:

1. **Agent-level:** The lead agent attempts resolution within scope
2. **Cross-agent:** Kabir coordinates if multiple agents are needed
3. **Founder-level:** Unresolved blockers escalate to the appropriate founder within 24 hours
4. **Client communication:** Arjun manages all client-facing escalation communication

### 9.3 Quality Standards

- Every deliverable must be reviewed by at least one agent besides the creator
- Client-facing documents require Tara's brand voice review
- Financial projections require Veer's validation
- Technical specifications require Ujjawal's architecture review
- All deliverables must reference the Discovery Brief to maintain alignment

## 11. Vertical Interconnection Map

The five verticals are designed to feed into each other, creating a natural client journey and compounding revenue:

```
Education (V1)
    └──→ Client gains AI awareness
         └──→ Consulting (V2)
              └──→ Client identifies automation opportunities
                   └──→ Development (V3)
                        └──→ Client has production automations
                             └──→ Maintenance (V4)
                                  └──→ Client retains ongoing support
                                       └──→ Pre-built (V5) add-ons

Pre-built Automations (V5)
    └──→ Quick deployment for common needs
         └──→ Client sees value, engages for custom work
              └──→ Development (V3)
                   └──→ Maintenance (V4)
```

**Key conversion paths:**

- Education to Consulting: 40-50% expected conversion (educated clients become informed buyers)
- Consulting to Development: 60-70% expected conversion (clear ROI drives commitment)
- Development to Maintenance: 70-80% expected conversion (clients want ongoing support for what they depend on)
- Pre-built to Development: 30-40% expected conversion (quick wins create trust for larger engagements)

## 12. Pricing Philosophy

While specific pricing is maintained separately by Veer (Pricing & Unit Economics Specialist), the following principles govern pricing across all verticals:

1. **Value-based, not cost-based:** Pricing reflects the business value delivered, not just the hours invested
2. **Transparent:** Clients understand what they pay for and why
3. **SME-accessible:** Entry points exist for businesses at every budget level
4. **ROI-anchored:** Every engagement is positioned against projected business impact
5. **Scalable:** Pricing models scale with client size and complexity without requiring renegotiation
6. **Bundling incentives:** Multi-vertical engagements receive preferential pricing to drive full-lifecycle adoption
