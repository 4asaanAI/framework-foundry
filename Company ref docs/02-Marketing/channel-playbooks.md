## 2. LinkedIn — Organic Thought Leadership

### 2.1 Strategy

LinkedIn is Layaa AI's primary awareness and trust-building channel. The strategy is founder-led: Abhimanyu and Shubham's personal brands drive reach and credibility, with the company page providing institutional content. This approach outperforms brand-only content by 3-5x in engagement within the Indian B2B space.

### 2.2 Account Structure

| Account | Role | Content Focus | Posting Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Abhimanyu Singh (Personal)** | Primary thought leader | AI adoption strategy, building in public, SME business insights, AI ethics | 4-5x/week |
| **Shubham Sharma (Personal)** | Technical thought leader | Automation architecture, AI reality checks, product insights, engineering culture | 3-4x/week |
| **Layaa AI (Company Page)** | Institutional presence | Product updates, case studies, team culture, event announcements | 3-4x/week |

1. AI Adoption for Indian SMEs (40%) — Practical advice, market observations, trends

**Shubham:**

**Layaa AI Company Page:**

### 2.5 Engagement Protocol

- **Respond to every comment** on own posts within 4 hours during business hours.
- **Comment on 5-10 relevant posts daily** from each founder account — genuine, substantive comments (no "Great post!" — always add an insight, question, or related experience).
- **Engage with ICP-relevant content** from potential clients, industry leaders, and complementary businesses.
- **Share and amplify** each other's content with added commentary (not just a reshare).
- **DM protocol:** Only DM after genuine engagement. Never cold-pitch. Open with a reference to their content or a shared connection.

## 3. Content Marketing — Blog, Case Studies, Whitepapers

### 3.1 Strategy

Content marketing serves two objectives: (1) SEO-driven inbound lead generation for prospects searching for AI automation solutions, and (2) credibility content that supports the sales process. All content must demonstrate practical expertise, not theoretical knowledge.

### 3.2 Content Types

| Type | Frequency | Length | Purpose | Funnel Stage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Blog Posts** | 2-4/month | 1,000-2,500 words | SEO, thought leadership, education | Top-to-Mid |
| **Case Studies** | 1-2/month | 800-1,200 words | Proof, sales enablement | Mid-to-Bottom |
| **Whitepapers/Guides** | 1/quarter | 3,000-5,000 words | Lead magnet, deep expertise | Top-to-Mid |
| **Quick Takes** | 2-4/month | 300-600 words | Timely reactions, SEO long-tail | Top |

### 3.3 Blog Content Calendar Structure

Each month should include:

| Week | Content | ICP Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Educational blog post (AI concept explained practically) | All |
| Week 2 | Case study or results-focused post | Rotating ICP |
| Week 3 | How-to or tactical guide | Rotating ICP |
| Week 4 | Opinion/thought leadership piece | All |

### 3.4 SEO Approach

**Keyword Strategy:**
- Target long-tail keywords specific to Indian SME AI adoption
- Primary clusters: "AI automation for [industry]," "AI for Indian SMEs," "[process] automation," "AI consulting India"
- Each blog post targets 1 primary keyword and 2-3 secondary keywords
- Write for readers first, search engines second

**On-Page Optimization:**
- Title tag: Primary keyword + benefit (under 60 characters)
- Meta description: 150-160 characters, compelling summary
- H2/H3 structure reflecting search intent
- Internal linking to related blog posts and product pages
- One clear CTA per post

**Technical SEO:**
- Fast page load (under 3 seconds)
- Mobile-optimized
- Schema markup for articles
- XML sitemap updated with every new post

### 3.5 Case Study Framework

Every case study follows this structure:

1. **Headline:** "[Result] for [Company Type]" (e.g., "60% Reduction in Filing Prep Time for Mumbai CA Firm")
2. **The Situation:** Company background, size, industry (2-3 sentences)
3. **The Challenge:** Specific pain point with quantified impact (3-4 sentences)
4. **The Solution:** What Layaa AI deployed and how (3-5 sentences)
5. **The Results:** Specific, measurable outcomes (bullet points with numbers)
6. **Client Quote:** Direct testimonial (if available)
7. **CTA:** "Facing a similar challenge? Talk to our team."

## 4. Email Marketing

### 4.1 Strategy

Email is the primary nurture and conversion channel. Every lead enters an email sequence designed to educate, build trust, and present relevant offers. Email is not used for cold outreach — all recipients have opted in through content downloads, event registration, or direct inquiry.

### 4.2 Email Sequences

**Sequence 1: Welcome (Triggered by: any lead magnet download or newsletter signup)**
- 5 emails over 21 days
- Structure: Welcome, Value, Case Study, Offer, Follow-up
- See content-templates.md for full templates

**Sequence 2: Post-Workshop (Triggered by: workshop attendance)**
- 4 emails over 14 days
- Structure: Thank You + Resources, Key Takeaway Deep-Dive, Case Study, Diagnostic Offer
- Higher intent; move to offer faster

**Sequence 3: Post-Diagnostic (Triggered by: completed diagnostic call)**
- 3 emails over 10 days
- Structure: Diagnostic Summary + Recommendations, ROI Breakdown, Pilot Proposal
- Highly personalized; references specific findings from the call

**Sequence 4: Re-engagement (Triggered by: no email opens in 60 days)**
- 2 emails over 14 days
- Structure: "Still relevant?" check-in, Final value offer or graceful unsubscribe
- Respects attention; doesn't spam

**Sequence 5: Client Nurture (Triggered by: active client, monthly)**
- Ongoing monthly newsletter for existing clients
- Structure: Product updates, tips for getting more from their automations, upcoming features, referral program reminder
- Goal: retention, expansion, referrals

### 4.3 Email Best Practices

| Practice | Standard |
|---|---|
| Send time | Tuesday-Thursday, 10:00-11:00 AM IST (tested optimal) |
| Subject line length | Under 50 characters |
| Preview text | Always customized; complements subject line |
| Personalization | First name minimum; company name and industry when available |
| Sender | Individual founder (Abhimanyu for business, Shubham for product) |
| Reply-to | Actual monitored inbox (not no-reply) |
| Unsubscribe | Prominent and easy; one-click |
| Mobile optimization | Required; 60%+ opens are mobile |
| A/B testing | Subject lines tested on every campaign (minimum 20% test audience) |

### 4.4 Metrics

| Metric | Target | Industry Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 35%+ | 25-30% (B2B India) |
| Click-through rate | 5%+ | 3-4% (B2B India) |
| Reply rate (nurture sequences) | 3%+ | 1-2% |
| Unsubscribe rate | <0.5% per email | <1% |
| Sequence completion rate | 70%+ | 50-60% |
| Leads converted from email | 5+/month | Varies |

## 5. Referral Program

### 5.1 Strategy

Satisfied clients are the highest-quality lead source. The referral program formalizes word-of-mouth by making it easy and rewarding for clients to refer businesses in their network.

### 5.2 Program Structure

**Who Can Refer:** Any active Layaa AI client with 30+ days of service.

**How It Works:**
1. Client shares a unique referral link or introduces via email.
2. Referred business books a diagnostic call.
3. If the referred business becomes a paying client, both parties receive a reward.

**Rewards:**
| Action | Reward for Referrer | Reward for Referred Business |
|---|---|---|
| Referred business books a diagnostic | Thank-you email + recognition | Free diagnostic (standard) |
| Referred business starts a paid engagement | 1 month free on their current plan OR ₹10,000 credit | 10% discount on first 3 months |
| 3+ successful referrals in a quarter | "Layaa AI Advocate" recognition + exclusive early access to new features | — |

### 5.3 Referral Activation Tactics

- **Monthly referral reminder** in the client nurture email sequence (soft, not pushy)
- **Post-milestone prompt:** After a client hits a significant result (e.g., "You've saved 100 hours this quarter!"), include a referral ask
- **QBR inclusion:** Reference the referral program in every Quarterly Business Review
- **Easy mechanics:** Pre-written email template and WhatsApp message the client can forward
- **Personal ask:** Account manager asks during regular check-ins: "Do you know anyone who could benefit from what we've done for you?"

### 5.4 Metrics

| Metric | Target (Monthly) |
|---|---|
| Referral links shared | 10+ |
| Referred diagnostics booked | 5+ |
| Referred conversions | 2+ |
| Referral as % of new clients | 20%+ (long-term target) |
| Referral program NPS | 8+ |

## 6. Events & Workshops

### 6.1 Strategy

Events and workshops serve as high-trust, high-engagement touchpoints that establish Layaa AI's expertise and generate qualified leads. The format ranges from virtual workshops (low cost, high reach) to in-person industry events (high cost, deep relationships).

### 6.2 Event Types

| Event Type | Format | Frequency | Audience Size | Primary Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AI for SMEs Workshop** | Virtual (Zoom/Google Meet) | Monthly | 20-50 | Education, lead gen |
| **Industry-Specific Workshop** | Virtual or hybrid | Quarterly | 15-30 | Targeted lead gen for specific ICP |
| **Founder Fireside Chat** | Virtual | Bi-monthly | 30-80 | Thought leadership, brand awareness |
| **Conference Speaking** | In-person | As opportunities arise | Varies | Brand awareness, credibility |
| **Private Roundtable** | In-person (invite-only) | Quarterly | 8-15 | Deep engagement with high-value prospects |

### 6.3 Workshop Playbook — "AI for SMEs" (Monthly)

**Pre-Event (2 weeks before):**
- Create event landing page with clear value proposition
- Promote via LinkedIn (organic posts from both founders + company page)
- Email invitation to relevant segments in database
- Personal outreach to 10-15 high-value prospects via DM or WhatsApp

**Event Structure (60-75 minutes):**
| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | 5 min | Welcome, agenda, housekeeping |
| Keynote | 15 min | Abhimanyu: State of AI adoption for Indian SMEs (insight-driven, not pitch) |
| Deep Dive | 20 min | Shubham: Live walkthrough of 2-3 automation examples relevant to the audience |
| Case Study | 10 min | Specific client result with before/after |
| Q&A | 15-20 min | Open questions from attendees |
| Close | 5 min | Recap, CTA (book a diagnostic), resource sharing |

**Post-Event (within 48 hours):**
- Send thank-you email with recording link and resources
- Trigger post-workshop email sequence
- Personally follow up with attendees who asked questions or stayed for the full session
- Share key insights from the workshop as LinkedIn content

### 6.4 Private Roundtable Playbook

**Format:** In-person, 8-15 senior decision-makers (CEO/MD/Ops Head level) from target ICPs.
**Duration:** 90 minutes + networking.
**Hosted by:** Abhimanyu (facilitator) + Shubham (technical resource).

**Structure:**
1. Brief introductions (each participant shares their biggest operational challenge)
2. Facilitated discussion on a specific theme (e.g., "AI adoption barriers for Indian SMEs")
3. Layaa AI shares relevant data and case studies (naturally, not as a pitch)
4. Open discussion and peer learning
5. Close with offer for private diagnostic conversations

**Goal:** Build relationships with 8-15 qualified prospects in a single event. Convert 2-3 to diagnostic calls within 30 days.

### 6.5 Metrics

| Metric | Target per Event |
|---|---|
| Registrations | 30-60 (virtual), 10-20 (roundtable) |
| Attendance rate | 50%+ (virtual), 80%+ (roundtable) |
| Engagement (questions asked, poll responses) | 30%+ of attendees |
| Post-event diagnostic calls booked | 3-5 (virtual), 2-3 (roundtable) |
| Leads entering nurture sequence | 80%+ of attendees |
| Event-attributed conversions (within 90 days) | 2-3 per event |

## 7. Partnerships

### 7.1 Strategy

Partnerships give Layaa AI access to established trust networks within target ICPs. Rather than building trust from scratch with each individual prospect, we embed within organizations and communities that our prospects already trust.

### 7.2 Partnership Types

| Partnership Type | Examples | Value to Layaa AI | Value to Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Industry Associations** | ICAI (CA association), CII, NASSCOM, logistics industry bodies | Access to member base, event speaking opportunities, credibility | AI education for members, modern relevance |
| **Complementary SaaS Companies** | Tally, Zoho, accounting software providers, logistics TMS vendors | Warm introductions to their client base; integration stories | Enhanced value proposition for their clients; co-marketing |
| **Consulting Firms (Non-AI)** | Management consultants, business advisors serving SMEs | Referral pipeline from firms that identify AI-ready clients | Ability to offer AI capabilities without building in-house |
| **Education & Training** | Business schools, professional development organizations | Workshop pipeline, credibility | Modern AI content for their curriculum |
| **Technology Partners** | Cloud providers, API platforms | Co-marketing opportunities, technical integration | Customer acquisition in Indian SME segment |

### 7.3 Partnership Engagement Process

1. **Identify:** Map potential partners in each category relevant to current ICP priority
2. **Qualify:** Assess alignment (audience overlap, complementary not competitive, mutual benefit)
3. **Approach:** Warm introduction preferred; cold outreach via founder with a specific value proposition
4. **Pilot:** Start with a single co-activity (co-hosted workshop, joint blog post, member webinar)
5. **Evaluate:** Measure leads generated, relationship quality, effort required
6. **Scale or Exit:** Formalize with regular cadence if pilot succeeds; gracefully step back if not

### 7.4 Partnership Cadence

| Activity | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Partner identification and outreach | Ongoing (2-3 new conversations/month) |
| Co-hosted events or workshops | 1 per quarter per active partner |
| Co-authored content | 1 piece per quarter per active partner |
| Partner review meeting | Quarterly per active partnership |
| Partnership pipeline review (internal) | Monthly |

### 7.5 Metrics

| Metric | Target (Quarterly) |
|---|---|
| Active partnerships | 3-5 |
| Partner-sourced leads | 10+ |
| Partner-sourced conversions | 3+ |
| Co-hosted events | 2+ |
| Partner satisfaction (informal feedback) | Positive / willing to continue |

## 8. Cross-Channel Integration

No channel operates in isolation. The following integration rules ensure a cohesive prospect experience:

### 8.1 Lead Flow

```
LinkedIn Content → Website / Blog → Lead Magnet Download → Email Sequence → Diagnostic Call → Pilot → Client

Workshop Registration → Workshop Attendance → Post-Workshop Sequence → Diagnostic Call → Pilot → Client

Partner Referral → Diagnostic Call → Pilot → Client

Client Referral → Diagnostic Call → Pilot → Client
```

### 8.2 Attribution Model

- **First-touch attribution:** Which channel first introduced the prospect to Layaa AI?
- **Last-touch attribution:** Which channel drove the diagnostic call or pilot signup?
- **Multi-touch tracking:** Track all touchpoints in CRM for pattern analysis

### 8.3 Content Repurposing Matrix

| Original Content | Repurposed Into |
|---|---|
| Blog post | 2-3 LinkedIn posts, 1 email newsletter segment, 1 Instagram reel script |
| Workshop recording | Blog post recap, 3-5 short video clips, quote graphics |
| Case study | LinkedIn post, email in nurture sequence, sales deck slide, website testimonial |
| Whitepaper | 4-6 blog posts, LinkedIn content series, email sequence, workshop topic |
| Founder LinkedIn post (high-performing) | Blog post expansion, email content, Instagram adaptation |

## 9. Quarterly Review Framework

Every quarter, review each channel against:

1. **Lead volume:** Are we generating enough leads from this channel?
2. **Lead quality:** Are leads from this channel converting at an acceptable rate?
3. **Cost efficiency:** What is the CAC from this channel compared to others?
4. **Effort required:** Is the time investment proportional to the output?
5. **Trajectory:** Is the channel improving or declining over time?

Reallocate budget and effort based on these findings. No channel is sacred — if it's not working after two full quarters of investment, reduce or pause.
