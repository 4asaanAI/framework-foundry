# Layaa AI — ICP & Market Context

## Primary Market
Indian SMEs (Small & Medium Enterprises), 10-200 employees, across technology-adjacent verticals willing to adopt AI/automation.

## Ideal Customer Profiles (4 Primary ICPs)

### ICP 1: SaaS Startups
- **Size:** 5-25 employees, post-seed stage
- **Primary Pain:** Manual ops slowing scale — founder doing everything
- **Typical Sales Cycle:** 30-50 days
- **Budget:** Medium-High (₹2-5L implementation)
- **Key Personas:** Founder/CEO, Head of Ops
- **Messaging Angle:** "Scale without hiring. Automate the repetitive so you can focus on product."
- **Common Objections:** "We'll build it in-house later" / "Is this enterprise-grade?"

### ICP 2: Logistics & Supply Chain SMEs
- **Size:** 50-200 employees
- **Primary Pain:** Manual tracking, inefficient coordination, paper-based workflows
- **Typical Sales Cycle:** 60-90 days
- **Budget:** ROI-driven (need clear cost savings proof)
- **Key Personas:** Operations Manager, Business Owner, Fleet/Warehouse Manager
- **Messaging Angle:** "Replace spreadsheets and phone calls with automated coordination."
- **Common Objections:** "Our processes are too unique" / "Team won't adopt new tools"

### ICP 3: Fintech & Payment Processors
- **Size:** 10-100 employees
- **Primary Pain:** Regulatory reporting burden, fraud detection gaps, manual compliance
- **Typical Sales Cycle:** 45-75 days
- **Budget:** Medium-High
- **Key Personas:** CTO, Compliance Head, Product Manager
- **Messaging Angle:** "Automate compliance and reporting so your team builds product, not paperwork."
- **Common Objections:** "Security concerns" / "Regulatory requirements make automation risky"

### ICP 4: Professional Services (HR/Legal/Consulting)
- **Size:** 10-50 employees
- **Primary Pain:** Admin overhead, client communication gaps, document-heavy workflows
- **Typical Sales Cycle:** 50-80 days
- **Budget:** Variable (start small, expand)
- **Key Personas:** Managing Partner, Practice Head, Office Manager
- **Messaging Angle:** "Automate admin so your experts spend time on billable work."
- **Common Objections:** "Our work is too nuanced for AI" / "Clients expect personal touch"

## Buyer Personas (from Zoya's Targeting Grid)

### Primary Personas (60-70% of targeting):
1. **The Overwhelmed Founder** — 1-50 employees, wearing multiple hats, time-starved, growth-minded but resource-constrained. Fast decision-maker. Budget: ₹15k-60k/month comfort zone.
2. **The Growth-Stage Founder** — 20-100 employees, scaling challenges, ready to invest in systems. Budget: ₹40k-1.2L/month.

### Secondary Personas (20-30%):
3. **The Operations Leader** — COO/VP Ops, 20-200 employees, efficiency-focused, data-driven.
4. **The Efficiency-Obsessed Manager** — Director-level, process improvement mandate.

## Competitive Landscape
- **Direct competitors:** Few in India's SME AI automation space (most serve enterprise)
- **Indirect competitors:** Freelance developers, internal IT teams, DIY tools
- **Layaa AI's edge:** Structured methodology, no-code/low-code delivery, ongoing support, India-specific pricing and context

## Market Context
- 63M+ MSMEs in India, ~30% GDP contribution
- Only 5-10% digitally mature — massive greenfield opportunity
- Government push (Digital India, DPIIT, Startup India) creating awareness
- AI adoption accelerating but most SMEs lack guidance on WHERE to start
